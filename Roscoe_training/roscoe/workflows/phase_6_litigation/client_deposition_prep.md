# Workflow: Client Deposition Preparation

## Phase: litigation
## Sub-Phase: depositions
## Goal: Thoroughly prepare client for deposition testimony

---

## When to Trigger

- Client deposition noticed
- Approaching deposition date
- User asks about deposition prep
- Client expresses concerns about deposition

---

## Inputs Required

- Deposition notice
- All discovery exchanged
- Medical records
- Client's prior statements
- Complaint and answer

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Schedule Preparation Sessions
Plan preparation time:
- Initial session: 2-3 hours
- Review session: 1-2 hours
- Day-before review: 30-60 minutes

### Step 2: Review Case Materials
With client, review:
1. Accident facts
2. Medical treatment
3. Prior medical history
4. Work history
5. Prior claims or lawsuits
6. Social media presence

### Step 3: Explain Deposition Process
**Use skill: client-deposition-prep**

Educate client on:
- Who will be present
- How questions work
- Role of court reporter
- Objections (and that client still answers)
- Breaks and duration
- Video recording (if applicable)

### Step 4: Fundamental Rules
Train client on:

1. **Listen Carefully**
   - Wait for complete question
   - Make sure you understand

2. **Answer Only What's Asked**
   - Don't volunteer
   - Don't explain
   - Don't guess

3. **Tell the Truth**
   - If you don't know, say so
   - If you don't remember, say so
   - Don't guess or estimate

4. **Take Your Time**
   - No rush
   - Think before answering
   - Okay to pause

5. **Watch for Tricks**
   - Leading questions
   - Compound questions
   - Assuming facts
   - "Always" and "never"

### Step 5: Review Key Topics
Practice questions on:
- Personal background
- Accident description
- Injuries and symptoms
- Medical treatment
- Current condition
- Prior injuries/conditions
- Work impact
- Daily life impact

### Step 6: Address Problem Areas
Identify and prepare for:
- Prior injuries to same body part
- Gaps in treatment
- Social media posts
- Inconsistent statements
- Criminal history
- Prior lawsuits

### Step 7: Document Review
Review with client:
- Accident report
- Medical records summaries
- Their prior written statements
- Discovery responses
- Photos/videos

### Step 8: Practice Session
Conduct mock examination:
- Ask difficult questions
- Practice proper responses
- Correct bad habits
- Build confidence

### Step 9: Day-Before Review
Quick refresher on:
- Location and time
- What to bring
- What to wear
- Key rules
- Emergency contact

### Step 10: Post-Deposition
After deposition:
1. Debrief with client
2. Order transcript
3. Review for errata
4. Note any issues

## Common Defense Questions

| Topic | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Background | Establish credibility baseline |
| Prior injuries | Undermine causation |
| Prior claims | Suggest litigation history |
| Social media | Find inconsistencies |
| Daily activities | Undermine damages |
| Treatment gaps | Undermine injury severity |

---

## Red Flags to Address

- Client tends to ramble
- Client gets defensive
- Client wants to explain everything
- Client has anger issues
- Client exaggerates
- Client's story has changed
