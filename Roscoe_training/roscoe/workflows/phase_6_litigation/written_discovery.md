# Workflow: Written Discovery

## Phase: litigation
## Sub-Phase: discovery
## Goal: Draft, propound, and respond to written discovery

---

## When to Trigger

- After answer filed
- Discovery period opens
- User requests discovery drafting
- Discovery responses due

---

## Inputs Required

- Complaint and answer
- Case facts
- Opposing party information
- Discovery deadlines
- Prior discovery (if any)

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Plan Discovery Strategy
**Use skill: discovery-drafting**

Determine what we need:
- Liability facts
- Damages information
- Insurance coverage
- Witness information
- Documents

### Step 2: Draft Interrogatories
Standard interrogatory topics:
1. Party identification
2. Witness identification
3. Factual contentions
4. Insurance coverage
5. Prior claims/lawsuits
6. Expert witnesses
7. Damages contentions
8. Document identification

### Step 3: Draft Requests for Production
Standard RFP topics:
1. Insurance policies
2. Incident reports
3. Photographs
4. Maintenance records
5. Training records
6. Communications
7. Medical authorizations (from defendant)
8. Prior claims documents

### Step 4: Draft Requests for Admission
Focus on:
- Undisputed facts
- Document authenticity
- Liability admissions (if appropriate)
- Damages admissions

### Step 5: Review and Finalize
1. Check against local rules (number limits)
2. Verify proper format
3. Include definitions and instructions
4. Attorney review and approval

### Step 6: Serve Discovery
1. Serve via permitted method
2. File certificate of service (if required)
3. Calendar response deadline
4. Note in case management

### Step 7: Track Responses
Monitor for:
- Timely responses
- Objections asserted
- Documents produced
- Supplementation needed

### Step 8: Respond to Defendant's Discovery
**Use skill: discovery-response**

When we receive discovery:
1. Calendar response deadline
2. Review requests
3. Gather responsive information
4. Draft responses and objections
5. Collect documents for production
6. Attorney review
7. Serve responses timely

### Step 9: Handle Objections
If responses are deficient:
1. Send meet and confer letter
2. Schedule conference
3. File motion to compel if needed
4. Document all efforts

### Step 10: Organize Productions
**Use skill: tar-protocol-proposal** (for large productions)

1. Bates stamp all documents
2. Create privilege log (if withholding)
3. Organize production logically
4. Maintain production index

---

## Skills Used

- **discovery-drafting**: Draft discovery requests
- **discovery-response**: Prepare responses
- **tar-protocol-proposal**: E-discovery protocols
- **document-organization**: Organize productions

---

## Completion Criteria

- [ ] Discovery strategy developed
- [ ] Interrogatories drafted and served
- [ ] RFPs drafted and served
- [ ] RFAs drafted and served (if appropriate)
- [ ] Responses to defendant's discovery served
- [ ] Productions organized
- [ ] Deficiency issues addressed

## Discovery Deadlines

| Event | Typical Deadline |
|-------|------------------|
| Initial Disclosures | 14 days after Rule 26(f) |
| Interrogatory Response | 30 days |
| RFP Response | 30 days |
| Expert Disclosure | Per scheduling order |
| Discovery Cutoff | Per scheduling order |

- Vague and ambiguous
- Overly broad
- Unduly burdensome
- Not reasonably calculated
- Privileged (specify)
