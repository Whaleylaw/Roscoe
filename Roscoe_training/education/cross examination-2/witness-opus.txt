## Step 2: Detailed Specifications for Each Report

---

### Report 1: Cross-Examination Fundamentals & Witness Control

**Purpose:**
This report establishes the core competencies for conducting cross-examination in civil litigation. It teaches the AI paralegal how to structure questions, maintain control over witnesses, handle evasive responses, and sequence impeachment for maximum jury impact.

**Intended Use in AI Paralegal OS:**
Used during deposition preparation, trial prep, and real-time assistance during examinations. The AI draws on these principles when drafting cross-examination outlines, suggesting follow-up questions, or identifying witness control problems in transcripts.

**Core Topics & Sections:**
- **Question Structure Fundamentals**
  - Leading questions only (declarative statements)
  - One fact per question rule
  - Prohibited question words (who, what, where, when, how, why, explain)
  - General to specific progression
- **Witness Control Techniques**
  - The "wait and stare" method
  - Word-for-word repetition
  - Using the court reporter strategically
  - "What question did I ask you?" redirect
  - The hand gesture technique
- **Handling Evasive Witnesses**
  - Types of evasion (non-responsive, volunteering, qualification)
  - What NOT to do (bargaining, "answer yes or no")
  - Effective techniques (repetition, slowing down, elimination method)
  - "Tell your story please" trap
- **Impeachment Methodology**
  - Listen to direct examination
  - Know and organize prior statements
  - Tie witness to direct (legally and emotionally)
  - Foundation elements (details, desire to tell truth)
  - Expose inconsistency with document citation
- **Cross-Examination Sequencing**
  - Start strong (gain control immediately)
  - Attack credibility and show bias early
  - Space impeachments throughout
  - Keep one strong point in reserve
  - End strong
- **Demonstrative Evidence**
  - Photos, diagrams, slides, flip charts
  - Physical demonstrations and quantities
  - Witness demeanor as evidence

**Source Coverage:**
Primarily drawn from Steve Romines' "Aggressive Cross Examination" seminar materials (Sections I-IX).

**Report Type Recommendation:**
**Mixed** – Training manual for conceptual understanding combined with practical checklist for real-time application.

### Report 2: Defense Medical Expert (IME) Cross-Examination

**Purpose:**
This report provides a comprehensive strategy for examining defense-retained medical experts, commonly called "Independent Medical Examiners." It teaches how to expose bias, challenge methodology, and obtain favorable concessions.

**Intended Use in AI Paralegal OS:**
Used when preparing for depositions or trial cross-examination of defense medical experts. The AI uses this to generate customized cross-examination outlines based on the specific expert's background, methodology, and opinions.

**Core Topics & Sections:**
- **Attacking "Independence"**
  - Court didn't pick, plaintiff didn't pick
  - Retained and paid by defense
  - No treatment relationship or follow-up care
  - Report sent only to defense
- **Financial Bias Exposure**
  - Volume of defense work (400-500 exams/year)
  - Percentage of income from litigation
  - Fee structures (exam, records review, report, deposition, trial)
  - Pattern of repeat defense work
- **Coaching by Defense Counsel**
  - Practice sessions and preparation meetings
  - "Do you need a coach to tell the truth to your wife?"
  - Time spent preparing testimony vs. examining patient
- **Methodology Challenges**
  - Time spent with patient
  - Tests performed vs. not performed
  - Records reviewed vs. ignored
  - Comparison to plaintiff's expert methodology
- **Expert Qualification Attacks**
  - Academic credentials and board certifications
  - Forensic vs. clinical experience
  - Lack of experience on specific issues
- **Obtaining Favorable Concessions**
  - "If you believe the client" hypotheticals
  - Cooperation and non-malingering admissions
  - Normal exam doesn't exclude pain
  - Chronic/permanent injury classifications
- **Background Investigation**
  - Professional license status and complaints
  - Criminal and civil court records
  - Insurance claims and losses
  - Prior testimony and publications

**Source Coverage:**
Drawn from Kyle Salyer's IME cross-examination scripts (pages 24-30) and Steve Romines' expert qualification/bias sections (Section XI).

**Report Type Recommendation:**
**Practical Checklist & Playbook** – Ready-to-use question sequences with strategic annotations.
