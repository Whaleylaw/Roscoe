## Core Principles

### 1. Control the Witness
- Use leading questions
- Short, simple questions
- One fact per question
- Don't ask "why"

### 2. Know Your Goals
- What must you establish?
- What admissions do you need?
- What documents will you use?
- What's your theory of the case?

### 3. Use Documents
- Confront with prior statements
- Use authoritative texts
- Reference medical records
- Show inconsistencies

---

## Question Structure

### Leading Questions
- "You did X, correct?"
- "Isn't it true that..."
- "You would agree that..."

### Control Techniques
- Loop back to unanswered questions
- Use documents as anchors
- Short questions get short answers
- Don't argue with the witness

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Asking open-ended questions
- Arguing with the witness
- Asking one question too many
- Not listening to answers
- Losing control of pace
- Showing frustration

---

## Outline Structure

```
I. TOPIC 1
   A. Establish foundation
      - Q: [Leading question]
      - Q: [Leading question]
   B. Get admission
      - Q: [Key question]
   C. Lock in with document
      - Show Exhibit X
      - Q: [Confirmation question]

II. TOPIC 2
   [Continue pattern]
```
