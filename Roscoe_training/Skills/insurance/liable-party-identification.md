# Liable Party Identification

## Skill Metadata

- **ID**: liable-party-identification
- **Category**: insurance
- **Tools Required**: read_file, write_file

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Setting up a new case
- Identifying who may be liable for injuries
- Determining insurance coverage for liable parties
- Analyzing complex liability scenarios

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Review Incident Facts

Gather information about:
- Type of incident (MVA, premises, product, etc.)
- Location of incident
- Parties present
- Property ownership
- Employment relationships

### Step 2: Identify Direct Tortfeasors

List all parties who may have directly caused injury:
- Vehicle drivers
- Property owners
- Product manufacturers
- Healthcare providers
- Employers

### Step 3: Identify Vicarious Liability

Check for:
- Employer liability (respondeat superior)
- Vehicle owner liability
- Franchisor liability
- Joint venture relationships
- Agency relationships

### Step 4: Identify Additional Parties

Consider:
- Government entities (notice requirements!)
- Contractors vs. employees
- Parent/subsidiary companies
- Insurance companies (direct action states)

### Step 5: Research Insurance Coverage

For each potential defendant:
1. Request insurance information
2. Verify coverage is active
3. Identify policy limits
4. Note excess/umbrella coverage

### Step 6: Document Findings

Create liability analysis documenting:
- Each potential defendant
- Basis for liability
- Known insurance
- Statute of limitations
- Notice requirements
