### Step-by-Step Process

#### **PHASE 1: Case Review & Strategy Refinement (6 Months Out)**

**Step 1.1 â€“ Comprehensive Case Assessment**
- Review all pleadings, motions, and court orders for completeness
- Identify all outstanding discovery or compliance issues
- Catalog case strengths and weaknesses based on completed discovery
- Update master case timeline with all known deadlines

**Step 1.2 â€“ Trial Theme Development**
- Assist attorney in articulating the core case narrative
- Identify 2-3 key themes that resonate with jury values
- Cross-reference theme against available evidence
- Document the trial theme for consistent messaging

**Step 1.3 â€“ Client Conference Preparation**
- Prepare case status summary for client meeting
- Draft questions regarding client's current condition/status
- Document client's expectations and concerns
- Update client contact information and availability

---

#### **PHASE 2: Fact Investigation & Discovery Completion (4-5 Months Out)**

**Step 2.1 â€“ Discovery Review & Completion**
- Audit all written discovery (interrogatories, RFPs, RFAs)
- Identify any outstanding responses or deficiencies
- Flag discovery disputes requiring motion practice
- Create discovery compliance checklist

**Step 2.2 â€“ Deposition Analysis**
- Create detailed summaries of all deposition transcripts
- Index key testimony by topic with page/line citations
- Identify contradictions and impeachment opportunities
- Flag testimony supporting each element of claims/defenses

**Step 2.3 â€“ Witness Inventory & Interviews**
- Update witness list with current contact information
- Confirm availability of all fact witnesses for trial dates
- Schedule follow-up interviews with key witnesses
- Document any changes in witness positions or willingness

**Step 2.4 â€“ Physical Evidence & Scene Documentation**
- Inventory all physical evidence
- Coordinate any necessary re-inspection of accident scenes
- Ensure chain of custody documentation is complete
- Photograph/document any deteriorating evidence

**Step 2.5 â€“ Expert Witness Coordination**
- Confirm all expert witness engagements
- Establish firm deadlines for final expert reports
- Obtain updated CVs and fee schedules
- Calendar expert report exchange deadlines

---

#### **PHASE 3: Pretrial Motions & Expert Finalization (2-3 Months Out)**

**Step 3.1 â€“ Dispositive Motion Analysis**
- Review any pending summary judgment motions
- Assist with research and document compilation for responses
- Track dispositive motion deadlines and hearing dates
- Document rulings and their impact on trial issues

**Step 3.2 â€“ Motions in Limine Preparation**
- Identify evidence to exclude (prejudicial, hearsay, irrelevant)
- Research legal basis for each proposed motion
- Draft motion in limine summaries for attorney review
- Track opposing counsel's expected motions in limine

**Step 3.3 â€“ Stipulation Development**
- Identify undisputed facts suitable for stipulation
- Draft proposed stipulations for attorney review
- Coordinate with opposing counsel on stipulation agreements
- Document all agreed stipulations for trial notebook

**Step 3.4 â€“ Expert Report Finalization**
- Ensure all expert reports are complete and exchanged
- Review reports for consistency with case theory
- Prepare expert report summaries for trial notebook
- Coordinate expert deposition scheduling if needed

---

#### **PHASE 4: Jury & Trial Logistics (Final 30 Days)**

**Step 4.1 â€“ Jury Research**
- Obtain jury pool list when available
- Conduct public records and social media research on potential jurors
- Create juror profiles with key demographics and potential biases
- Develop juror scoring system (green/yellow/red)

**Step 4.2 â€“ Voir Dire Preparation**
- Draft preliminary voir dire questions organized by topic:
  - Background/rapport questions
  - Knowledge and experience questions
  - Bias identification questions
  - Burden of proof education questions
- Create juror tracking chart for use during selection
- Prepare visual aids explaining burden of proof

**Step 4.3 â€“ Jury Instructions & Verdict Form**
- Research pattern jury instructions for jurisdiction
- Draft proposed jury instructions
- Prepare proposed verdict form
- Identify any contested instruction issues

**Step 4.4 â€“ Opening Statement & Closing Argument Support**
- Assist with opening statement outline
- Organize visual aids and demonstrative exhibits
- Compile key evidence citations for closing argument
- Create exhibit reference list keyed to argument themes

**Step 4.5 â€“ Witness Examination Preparation**
- Prepare direct examination outlines for all witnesses
- Develop cross-examination outlines for adverse witnesses
- Create impeachment packets with deposition citations
- Compile exhibit lists keyed to each witness

**Step 4.6 â€“ Trial Notebook Assembly**

Organize the following sections (digital and/or physical):

| Section | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| **Tab 1: Pleadings** | Complaint, Answer, Amended Pleadings |
| **Tab 2: Court Orders** | Scheduling Orders, Key Rulings |
| **Tab 3: Motions** | Motions in Limine (filed & rulings) |
| **Tab 4: Voir Dire** | Questions, Jury Research, Tracking Chart |
| **Tab 5: Opening/Closing** | Outlines, Visual Aids |
| **Tab 6: Witnesses** | Direct/Cross Outlines, Subpoenas |
| **Tab 7: Depositions** | Summaries, Key Excerpts with Citations |
| **Tab 8: Exhibits** | Master List, Pre-Marked Exhibits |
| **Tab 9: Jury Instructions** | Proposed Instructions, Verdict Form |
| **Tab 10: Legal Research** | Trial Briefs, Case Law |

**Step 4.7 â€“ Exhibit Management Protocol**
- Create comprehensive master exhibit list
- Cross-reference exhibits with authenticating witnesses
- Coordinate pre-marking with opposing counsel
- Identify exhibits for stipulated admission
- Prepare authentication question outlines for contested exhibits
- Create multiple exhibit copies (judge, clerk, witness, team)
- Prepare real-time exhibit tracking sheet

**Step 4.8 â€“ Logistics & Technology**
- Reserve war room/workspace near courthouse
- Confirm courtroom technology requirements
- Test all presentation equipment and software
- Prepare backup systems (paper exhibits, spare laptop)
- Coordinate team schedules and daily meeting times

---

#### **PHASE 5: Trial Execution Support**

**Step 5.1 â€“ Daily Trial Preparation**
- Review previous day's testimony and rulings
- Update exhibit tracking status
- Prepare materials for day's witnesses
- Coordinate witness arrival and waiting arrangements

**Step 5.2 â€“ Real-Time Courtroom Support**

**Step 5.4 â€“ Jury Selection Support**

- Research jurors as needed during selection
- Maintain strike list and challenge tracking

#### **PHASE 6: Post-Trial Activities**

**Step 6.1 â€“ Immediate Post-Verdict Tasks**

### Quality Checks & Safeguards

#### **Pre-Trial Quality Checks**
- [ ] All deadlines calendared with reminder notifications
- [ ] Witness contact information verified within 30 days of trial
- [ ] All exhibits pre-marked and copies prepared
- [ ] Backup technology systems tested
- [ ] Motion in limine rulings summarized for each witness
- [ ] Trial notebook reviewed for completeness
- [ ] Client prepared and available for all trial dates

#### **Red Flags Requiring Immediate Escalation**

| Red Flag | Risk Level | Required Action |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Witness unavailable/hostile** | CRITICAL | Escalate immediately; may require continuance or alternative evidence |
| **Exhibit authentication failure** | HIGH | Alert attorney; identify alternative authentication path |
| **Motion in limine violation** | CRITICAL | Immediate objection required; potential mistrial |
| **Undisclosed evidence introduced** | CRITICAL | Object immediately; request court intervention |
| **Technology failure without backup** | HIGH | Implement contingency plan; notify court if delay needed |
| **Client testimony contradicts prior statements** | CRITICAL | Immediate sidebar with attorney required |
| **Key deadline missed** | CRITICAL | Escalate for emergency motion or waiver analysis |
| **Juror misconduct observed** | HIGH | Document and alert attorney immediately |

#### **Validation Checks During Trial**
- [ ] Daily exhibit status reconciliation
- [ ] Witness schedule confirmed 24 hours in advance
- [ ] All equipment tested before each session
- [ ] Juror note tracking current
- [ ] Key testimony documented with citations

#### **When to Escalate to Supervising Attorney**
- Any red flag condition identified above
- Witness demands payment for testimony
- Discovery of previously unknown adverse evidence
- Settlement overtures from opposing counsel
- Juror questions suggesting confusion or bias
- Any situation requiring legal judgment or strategic decision
