# Expert Witness Preparation & Examination

## Operational Workflow

### Workflow Name
**Expert Witness Preparation & Examination Workflow**

---

### Goal
Successfully prepare expert witnesses for deposition and trial testimony by:
- Documenting complete testimonial history and credibility factors
- Creating comprehensive deposition exhibit packages
- Establishing clear standard of care articulation
- Preparing thorough cross-examination materials for defense experts
- Tracking all expert fees, billing, and case involvement

---

### When to Use

**Trigger Conditions:**
- Expert witness is retained for a case (plaintiff's expert)
- Preparing for deposition of plaintiff's expert
- Preparing to depose defense expert (IME doctor, liability expert, etc.)
- Trial preparation phase involving expert testimony
- Expert disclosure deadline approaching (CR 26.02 or equivalent)
- Need to evaluate potential expert's testimonial history before retention

---

### Inputs Required

| Input Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Expert CV/Resume | Complete curriculum vitae with publications, presentations, and credentials |
| Expert Engagement Agreement | Retention letter, fee schedule, scope of work |
| Case File | Relevant pleadings, discovery, medical records, or technical documents |
| Prior Testimony Records | Transcripts from previous depositions/trials (if available) |
| Billing Records | Expert's invoices and time entries for current case |

| Testimonial History Database | Information on expert's prior case involvement |

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Phase 1: Expert Intake & Documentation

**Step 1.1: Generate Expert Intake Questionnaire**
- Request complete CV with updates
- Document all professional licenses (state, status, disciplinary history)
- Identify board certifications and specialty training
- Catalog teaching positions, hospital affiliations
- List all publications relevant to case issues

**Step 1.2: Compile Testimonial History**
- Determine when expert first began forensic/litigation work
- Calculate percentage of work for plaintiff vs. defense
- Document total number of cases reviewed in litigation context
- Count depositions given (last 4 years minimum)
- Count trial testimony appearances
- Calculate annual income from forensic/litigation work
- Determine percentage of total income from litigation work

**Step 1.3: Assess Expert Credibility Factors**
- Research malpractice claims history
- Verify license status in all jurisdictions
- Check expert witness clearinghouse listings (IDEX, etc.)
- Review any published criticisms or sanctions
- Document any board disciplinary actions

**Step 1.4: Analyze Prior Relationships**
- Identify any prior work with same insurance carrier
- Document prior engagements with opposing counsel
- Note any prior adverse testimony against current client's position
- Check for conflicts of interest

---

#### Phase 2: Deposition Exhibit Preparation

**Step 2.1: Assemble Complete Exhibit Package**

Create organized exhibit binder containing:

| Exhibit Category | Contents |
|------------------|----------|
| Correspondence | All letters, emails between expert and counsel |
| Billing Records | Complete invoices, time entries, payment history |
| Notes & Work Product | Handwritten notes, draft reports, annotations |
| Images & Demonstratives | Photos, diagrams, charts created or reviewed |
| Testimonial History | Summary of prior litigation involvement |
| CR 26.02 Disclosure | Expert disclosure document (or equivalent rule) |
| Publications | Relevant articles, books, chapters authored by expert |
| Prior Testimony | Transcripts from previous cases on similar issues |

**Step 2.2: Create Document Index**
- Bates-number all exhibits
- Create master index with exhibit descriptions
- Flag documents for specific examination topics
- Note any privileged materials requiring protection

---

#### Phase 3: Standard of Care & Causation Documentation

**Step 3.1: Standard of Care Definition Protocol**
- Work with expert to articulate standard of care in **2-3 clear sentences**
- Document the specific standard applicable to defendant's specialty
- Identify authoritative sources supporting the standard (textbooks, guidelines, peer-reviewed literature)
- Prepare expert to explain standard in lay terms for jury

**Step 3.2: Substantial Factor Causation Framework (Kentucky Standard)**
- Document how defendant's conduct was a "substantial factor" in causing injury
- Distinguish from "but-for" causation where applicable
- Prepare expert to explain causation chain clearly
- Identify any competing causes and expert's response to same
- Document temporal relationship between breach and injury

**Step 3.3: Prepare Questioning Framework**
- Draft direct examination questions on standard of care
- Draft direct examination questions on causation
- Anticipate cross-examination challenges
- Prepare redirect topics

---

#### Phase 4: Current Case Workload Documentation

**Step 4.1: Document Active Case Involvement**
- List all other pending cases where expert is retained
- Identify issues in other cases (potential consistency questions)
- Note which side expert represents in each case
- Document any cases involving same parties, counsel, or carriers

**Step 4.2: Track Time & Availability**
- Confirm expert's availability for trial dates
- Document time spent on current case
- Track deadlines for reports and disclosures

---

#### Phase 5: Defense Expert Cross-Examination Preparation

**Step 5.1: Bias Indicator Analysis**
- Calculate defense expert's plaintiff/defense case ratio
- Document percentage of income from defense work
- Identify repeat engagements with same defense counsel
- Note repeat engagements with same insurance carriers
- Research expert's relationship with defense medical organizations

**Step 5.2: Case Volume Analysis**
- Document number of IMEs/reviews performed annually
- Calculate average time spent per case
- Compare to time spent on actual patient care
- Identify any "expert mill" indicators

**Step 5.3: Credibility Attack Preparation**
- Research prior testimony inconsistent with current opinions
- Identify publications contradicting current position
- Document any sanctions, license issues, malpractice history
- Prepare impeachment materials

**Step 5.4: Substantive Cross-Examination Topics**
- Challenge basis for opinions (incomplete record review, etc.)
- Question methodology and adherence to scientific standards
- Explore alternative explanations not considered
- Test expert's knowledge of relevant literature

---

#### Phase 6: Expert Publications & Lectures Review

**Step 6.1: Catalog Relevant Publications**
- Identify all publications on issues relevant to case
- Flag any statements that support or contradict current opinions
- Prepare to use favorable publications on direct
- Anticipate defense use of unfavorable publications

**Step 6.2: Review Lecture Materials**
- Obtain slides, handouts from relevant presentations
- Document any recorded statements on case issues
- Identify continuing education courses taught

### Quality Checks & Safeguards

#### Validation Checks

| Check | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| License Verification | Confirm active, unrestricted license in relevant jurisdiction |
| CV Accuracy | Cross-reference CV claims against verifiable sources |
| Testimonial Consistency | Compare current opinions to prior testimony |
| Disclosure Completeness | Ensure CR 26.02 disclosure contains all required elements |
| Billing Accuracy | Verify time entries against work product |
| Conflict Check | Confirm no undisclosed relationships with opposing parties |

#### Red Flags Requiring Attorney Escalation

- Expert has undisclosed malpractice claims or license issues
- Testimonial history shows significant inconsistencies
- Expert's opinions contradict their own publications
- Expert cannot articulate standard of care clearly
- Expert's case volume suggests inadequate time per case
- Undisclosed prior relationship with opposing party/counsel
- Expert requests unusual fee arrangements or contingent fees
- Expert's methodology does not meet Daubert/reliability standards
- Expert refuses to produce required documents for disclosure

#### Escalation Protocol

1. **Immediate escalation** for ethical issues (conflicts, undisclosed relationships)
2. **Prompt escalation** for credibility concerns that may affect case strategy
3. **Routine escalation** for scheduling conflicts or administrative issues
4. Document all red flags with supporting evidence for attorney review

### Defense Expert Cross-Examination Framework
1. **Bias**: Income dependency, repeat relationships
2. **Volume**: Cases per year, time per case
3. **Credibility**: License, malpractice, sanctions
4. **Substance**: Methodology, completeness, consistency

### Standard of Care Protocol
> Expert must articulate standard in **2-3 clear sentences** that:
> - Define the applicable specialty standard
> - Identify what a reasonably prudent practitioner would do
> - Are supported by authoritative sources

### Kentucky Substantial Factor Causation
> Defendant's conduct must be a "substantial factor" in bringing about the harm. Prepare expert to:
> - Explain the causation chain
> - Address competing causes
> - Establish temporal relationship
