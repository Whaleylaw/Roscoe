- **Tools Required**: read_file, write_file, internet_search

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Defense has designated a medical expert
- Preparing to depose DME/IME doctor
- Cross-examining defense expert at trial
- Need to challenge adverse medical opinions

---

## Pre-Deposition Research

### Research the Expert

Use `internet_search` and `expert_witness_lookup` tools:
1. Publication history
2. Prior testimony history
3. Income from defense work
4. Professional background
5. Board certifications
6. Any disciplinary actions

### Key Questions to Research
- How much does expert earn from defense work?
- How many exams per year?
- Percentage of work for defense?
- Prior inconsistent testimony?
- Publications contradicting current opinion?

---

## Cross-Examination Areas

### 1. Bias and Financial Interest
- Annual income from litigation work
- Percentage defense vs. plaintiff
- Relationship with defense attorneys
- Referral sources
- Number of exams performed

### 2. Limited Examination
- Time spent with patient
- Tests NOT performed
- Records NOT reviewed
- History NOT obtained
- Comparison to treating physician time

### 3. Cherry-Picking Records
- What records were reviewed?
- What records were NOT reviewed?
- Reliance on incomplete information
- Ignoring contradictory evidence

### 4. Methodology Challenges
- Basis for opinions
- Acceptance in medical community
- Deviation from standards
- Lack of testing

### 5. Contradictions
- Inconsistent with own writings
- Inconsistent with prior testimony
- Inconsistent with medical literature
- Inconsistent with treating doctors

## Sample Questions

### Financial Bias
```
Q: You earn income from performing IMEs, correct?
Q: Approximately how much did you earn from IMEs last year?
Q: The majority of your IME work is for defendants, isn't it?
Q: You've performed IMEs for this law firm before?
Q: If you found in favor of plaintiffs, you'd get fewer referrals, wouldn't you?
```

### Limited Exam
```
Q: You spent approximately 30 minutes with [Client], correct?
Q: Her treating doctor has seen her over 20 times?
Q: You did not perform [specific test], did you?
Q: You did not review [specific record], correct?
```

### Cherry-Picking
```
Q: You noted the MRI showed [finding], correct?
Q: But you didn't mention [other finding] in your report?
Q: Dr. [Treating] found [positive finding], didn't he?
Q: That's not in your report, is it?
```
