# 30(b)(6) Topic Drafting

## Skill Metadata

- **ID**: 30b6-topic-drafting
- **Category**: depositions
- **Reference Material**: education/depositions/
- **Tools Required**: read_file, write_file

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Deposing a corporation or organization
- Need testimony on corporate knowledge
- Need to bind the organization
- Seeking information beyond individual knowledge

---

## Rule 30(b)(6) Basics

### What It Does
- Requires organization to designate witness(es)
- Witness testifies on behalf of organization
- Organization bound by testimony
- Must prepare witness on all noticed topics

### Key Benefits
- Can't hide behind "I don't know"
- Binds the organization
- Prevents witness-shopping
- Forces preparation

---

## Topic Drafting Guidelines

### Be Specific
- Narrow, defined topics
- Time periods specified
- Documents identified
- Events described

### Be Comprehensive
- Cover all needed areas
- Don't leave gaps
- Consider all knowledge areas

### Be Reasonable
- Topics must be ascertainable
- Don't make overbroad
- Court will narrow if needed

---

## Common Topic Areas

### Corporate Defendant Topics
1. Corporate structure and history
2. Policies and procedures relevant to incident
3. Training programs
4. Prior similar incidents
5. Investigation of incident
6. Document retention
7. Insurance coverage
8. Witnesses with knowledge

### Insurance Company Topics
1. Claim handling procedures
2. Investigation of claim
3. Basis for coverage decisions
4. Reserve setting
5. Communications about claim
6. Authority levels

---

## Topic Structure

```
Topic 1: [Title]

[Organization's] knowledge, policies, procedures, and practices 
concerning [specific subject matter], including but not limited to:

(a) [Specific sub-topic];
(b) [Specific sub-topic];
(c) [Specific sub-topic];

for the time period [date range].
```

---

## Sample Topics

### Example: MVA Against Trucking Company

```
Topic 3: Driver Hiring and Training

Defendant's policies, procedures, and practices regarding the 
hiring, training, qualification, and supervision of commercial 
drivers, including:

(a) Minimum qualifications for hire;
(b) Background check procedures;
(c) Initial training programs;
(d) Ongoing training requirements;
(e) Safety record monitoring;
(f) Disciplinary procedures;

as applied to driver [Name] and generally for the period 
January 1, 2020 through present.
```
