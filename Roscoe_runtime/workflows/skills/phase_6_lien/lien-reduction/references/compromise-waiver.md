# Medicare Compromise/Waiver Reference

## Overview

When Medicare's standard procurement cost reduction isn't sufficient, beneficiaries can request a compromise or waiver of the Medicare lien. This is a formal process with specific requirements.

---

## Types of Relief

### 1. Compromise

**Definition:** Medicare accepts less than the full amount owed.

**When Available:**
- Full payment would cause financial hardship
- Beneficiary has limited resources
- Collection would be cost-prohibitive

### 2. Waiver

**Definition:** Medicare waives the entire debt.

**When Available:**
- Beneficiary without fault in creating debt
- Recovery would defeat purpose of benefits
- Recovery would be against equity and good conscience

---

## Compromise Process

### Step 1: Determine Eligibility

**Consider:**
- Net to beneficiary after lien
- Beneficiary's financial situation
- Total recovery vs. total damages
- Ongoing medical needs

### Step 2: Calculate Hardship

**Financial Factors:**
- Income
- Assets
- Ongoing medical expenses
- Other debts

**Recovery Factors:**
- Settlement as percentage of damages
- Net after attorney fees and costs
- Adequacy of net for ongoing needs

### Step 3: Prepare Compromise Request

**Required Information:**
1. Settlement breakdown
2. Beneficiary financial information
3. Explanation of hardship
4. Proposed compromise amount
5. Documentation supporting request

### Step 4: Submit to BCRC

**Submit to:**
```
Medicare Secondary Payer Recovery Contractor
PO Box 138899
Oklahoma City, OK 73113-8899
```

**Or call:** 1-855-798-2627

### Step 5: Await Determination

**Timeline:** 30-90 days typically

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Approved as requested
- Counter-offer
- Denied

## Compromise Request Contents

### Required Elements

```
RE: Medicare Lien Compromise Request
    Beneficiary: [Name]
    HICN/MBI: [Number]
    Date of Injury: [Date]

1. SETTLEMENT INFORMATION
   - Settlement Amount: $[amount]
   - Attorney Fee: $[fee]
   - Costs: $[costs]
   - Other Liens: $[liens]
   - Medicare Lien: $[medicare]
   - Net to Beneficiary: $[net]

2. HARDSHIP EXPLANATION
   [Detailed explanation of why full payment 
   creates hardship]

3. FINANCIAL INFORMATION
   - Monthly Income: $[amount]
   - Assets: $[amount]
   - Monthly Expenses: $[amount]
   - Other Debts: $[amount]

4. MEDICAL NEEDS
   [Description of ongoing medical needs]

5. COMPROMISE REQUEST
   We respectfully request Medicare accept 
   $[proposed] in full satisfaction.

6. SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION
   - Settlement statement
   - Financial statements
   - Medical records showing ongoing needs
```

|----------|---------|

### Medical Documents

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Medical records | Show ongoing treatment |
| Doctor statements | Prognosis and needs |
| Bills | Show ongoing expenses |

## Arguments for Compromise

### 1. Inadequate Net Recovery

> "After payment of the Medicare lien, the beneficiary would receive only $[net], which is insufficient to address ongoing medical needs and living expenses."

### 2. Limited Settlement Recovery

> "The settlement of $[amount] represents only [X]% of the total damages, demonstrating the limited recovery available in this case."

### 3. Ongoing Medical Needs

> "The beneficiary requires ongoing medical treatment for [conditions], and depleting the settlement for lien payment would leave them unable to afford necessary care."

### 4. Fixed Income

> "The beneficiary lives on fixed income of $[amount]/month and has no other resources to address ongoing needs."

---

## Appeal Process

If compromise denied:

### Administrative Review
- Request within 120 days
- Submit additional documentation
- Address reasons for denial

### ALJ Hearing
- If administrative review unsuccessful
- More formal proceeding
- May require attorney involvement
