## Point of Impact (Clock Position)

The point of initial impact on a vehicle is often described using clock positions:
- **12** = Front center
- **1-2** = Front right
- **3** = Right side center
- **4-5** = Right rear
- **6** = Rear center
- **7-8** = Left rear
- **9** = Left side center
- **10-11** = Left front

## Notes on Reading Kentucky Reports

1. **Unit Numbers**: Unit 1 is typically listed first, Unit 2 second, etc.

2. **Owner vs Driver**: Critical for PIP. If "Driver is Owner" is NO, owner name should be listed separately.

3. **Insurance Section**: May be blank if driver couldn't provide at scene. Follow up needed.

4. **Narrative**: Officer's description of what happened. Most important section for liability.

5. **Diagram**: Shows vehicle positions, direction of travel, and point of impact.

6. **Contributing Factors**: Multiple codes may be listed. Primary factor is most relevant.

7. **Citations**: Listed separately. Critical for liability determination.
