# Deposition Outline Reference

## Standard PI Defendant Deposition Structure

### I. Introduction and Background

**Purpose:** Establish identity, assess credibility baseline

```
1. Full legal name, date of birth
2. Current address, how long there
3. Education
4. Employment history
5. Driving history (MVA cases)
6. Prior accidents/claims
7. Prior lawsuits
```

### II. Pre-Accident Information

**Purpose:** Establish what defendant was doing before accident

```
1. Where were you coming from?
2. Where were you going?
3. What time did you leave?
4. Expected arrival time?
5. Were you in a hurry?
6. Route taken - familiar?
7. Any phone use before accident?
8. Any distractions?
9. Weather conditions awareness
10. Vehicle condition
```

### III. The Accident

**Purpose:** Get defendant's version locked in

```
1. First awareness of plaintiff
2. What were you doing immediately before?
3. Speed at various points
4. Actions taken to avoid
5. Point of impact
6. Describe the collision
7. Movements of vehicles after impact
8. Where did vehicles come to rest?
9. Get out of vehicle?
10. First thing you did after stopping?
```

### IV. Post-Accident

**Purpose:** Establish admissions, statements, investigation

```
1. Who did you speak to at scene?
2. What did you tell them?
3. Any apologies or admissions?
4. Police arrive?
5. Statement to police?
6. Did you see plaintiff?
7. Plaintiff's apparent condition?
8. Ambulance called?
9. Your injuries?
10. Your vehicle damage?
```

### V. Investigation and Insurance

**Purpose:** Identify witnesses, understand defense preparation

```
1. Report accident to insurance?
2. Who did you speak to?
3. What did you tell them?
4. Investigated by anyone?
5. Provide statement?
6. Written or recorded?
7. Photographs taken?
8. Vehicle inspected?
9. Anyone visit accident scene?
```

### VI. Contentions

**Purpose:** Pin down defense theory

```
1. Do you contend plaintiff did anything wrong?
2. What specifically?
3. Do you contend plaintiff was speeding?
4. Basis for that contention?
5. Any witnesses to support?
6. Do you deny causing the accident?
7. What is your explanation?
```

### VII. Documents and Exhibits

**Purpose:** Authenticate documents, get admissions

```
1. Show accident report - accurate?
2. Show photos - recognize?
3. Show vehicle - yours?
4. Any documents you prepared?
5. Any texts/emails about accident?
```

## 30(b)(6) Corporate Deposition

### Topics to Notice

```
1. Policies and procedures relevant to [subject]
2. Training of employees
3. Maintenance and inspection records
4. Prior similar incidents
5. Investigation of this incident
6. Insurance coverage
7. Corporate structure and decision-makers
```

### Questioning Strategy

- Establish witness's preparation
- Confirm speaking for company
- Ask about company's official position
- Get admissions binding on corporation

## Expert Deposition Topics

```
1. Qualifications and experience
2. Retention and compensation
3. Materials reviewed
4. Methodology used
5. Opinions formed
6. Bases for opinions
7. Alternative explanations considered
8. Prior testimony on this topic
9. Daubert factors (reliability)
```

## Questioning Techniques

### Open Questions First
- "Tell me about..."
- "Describe..."
- "What happened next?"

### Then Narrow
- "When exactly?"
- "Who specifically?"
- "How far?"

### Then Confirm
- "So it's your testimony that..."
- "Is that a yes?"
- "Anything else?"

### Looping
- Repeat key admissions
- Connect to other testimony
- Lock in for impeachment
