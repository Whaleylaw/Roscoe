# Direct Examination Reference

## Purpose of Direct

- Present facts through witness
- Build credibility
- Introduce exhibits
- Tell your story

---

## Basic Structure

### 1. Introduction

```
Q: Please state your name for the jury.
Q: Where do you live?
Q: How are you employed?
Q: How long have you been doing that?
```

### 2. Background

Build credibility:
```
Q: Tell us about your education.
Q: What training have you received?
Q: How long have you been [relevant experience]?
```

### 3. Scene Setting

```
Q: Where were you on [date]?
Q: What were you doing?
Q: Who was with you?
Q: What time was this?
```

### 4. The Event

```
Q: What happened next?
Q: What did you see?
Q: What did you hear?
Q: Then what happened?
```

### 5. Conclusion

```
Q: What injuries did you suffer?
Q: How has this affected your life?
Q: What are you asking the jury to do?
```

---

## Question Types

### Open Questions (Primary)

- Who
- What
- Where
- When
- How
- Describe
- Tell us
- Explain

### Transitional Questions

```
Q: Let me direct your attention to [topic].
Q: Now I want to ask you about [topic].
Q: Moving forward to [time/event].
```

### Exhibit Questions

```
Q: I'm showing you what's been marked as Exhibit [X].
Q: Do you recognize this?
Q: What is it?
Q: Is this a fair and accurate representation of [subject]?
```

---

## Introducing Exhibits

### Documents

```
Q: I'm showing you Exhibit 1. Do you recognize this?
A: Yes, it's [description].
Q: How do you recognize it?
A: [Explains].
Q: Is this a true and accurate copy?
A: Yes.
COUNSEL: Your Honor, I move to admit Exhibit 1.
```

### Photographs

```
Q: I'm showing you Exhibit 2. Do you recognize what's 
   depicted in this photograph?
A: Yes, that's [subject].
Q: Does this photograph fairly and accurately depict 
   [subject] as it appeared on [date]?
A: Yes.
COUNSEL: I move to admit Exhibit 2.
```

## Common Witnesses

### Plaintiff

- Background and character
- Day of accident
- Injuries and impact
- Current condition

### Treating Physician

- Qualifications
- Treatment provided
- Opinions on causation
- Prognosis

### Lay Witness

- Relationship to plaintiff
- Observations before/after
- Specific examples of impact

---

## Tips

### Do:
- Listen to answers
- Follow up on important points
- Vary pace and tone
- Let witness tell story
- Use exhibits effectively

### Don't:
- Ask leading questions
- Rush the witness
- Interrupt answers
- Repeat answers
- Argue with witness
