# Counterclaim Handling Reference

## Overview

A counterclaim is a claim by defendant against plaintiff. When filed with the answer, plaintiff must respond within 20 days.

---

## Types of Counterclaims

### Compulsory Counterclaim (CR 13.01)

**Definition:** Arises from same transaction/occurrence as plaintiff's claim.

**Rule:** Must be filed with answer or waived.

**Example in PI:** Defendant claims plaintiff caused the accident.

### Permissive Counterclaim (CR 13.02)

**Definition:** Any claim against plaintiff, even unrelated.

**Rule:** May be filed with answer or separately.

**Example:** Unrelated debt claim.

---

## Response Process

### Step 1: Calculate Deadline

Response due 20 days from service of counterclaim.

### Step 2: Analyze Counterclaim

| Element | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| Type | Compulsory or Permissive |
| Claims | What causes of action |
| Damages | What is sought |
| Validity | Is claim legally viable |

### Step 3: Prepare Response

Options:
- **Answer:** Admit/deny allegations
- **Motion to Dismiss:** If legally deficient
- **Motion to Strike:** If improper
- **Motion to Sever:** If unrelated permissive counterclaim

---

## Common PI Counterclaims

### Property Damage

**Defendant claims:** Plaintiff damaged their vehicle/property.

**Response considerations:**
- Liability still contested
- Offset against plaintiff's damages
- May not be worth contesting if small

### Personal Injury

**Defendant claims:** They were also injured.

**Response considerations:**
- Get their medical records
- Investigate their treatment
- May affect comparative fault

### Abuse of Process / Malicious Prosecution

**Defendant claims:** Lawsuit is frivolous.

**Response considerations:**
- Usually fails if any merit to claim
- May indicate scorched earth defense
- Document good faith basis for suit

---

## Discovery on Counterclaims

Now entitled to discover:
- Defendant's damages
- Defendant's injuries
- Defendant's medical records (if claiming injury)
- Defendant's property damage evidence

---

## Insurance Considerations

- Notify client's insurance of counterclaim
- May trigger duty to defend
- Coordinate with insurance defense counsel if appointed

## Strategic Considerations

| Situation | Strategy |
|-----------|----------|
| Small counterclaim | May be worth settling to simplify |
| Large counterclaim | Vigorously defend |
| Compulsory counterclaim | Expect it; prepare accordingly |
| Frivolous counterclaim | Consider sanctions motion |
