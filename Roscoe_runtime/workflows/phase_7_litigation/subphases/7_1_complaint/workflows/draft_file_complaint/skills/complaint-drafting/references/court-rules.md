# Kentucky Court Rules Reference

## Civil Rule 8 - General Rules of Pleading

### Claims for Relief (CR 8.01)

A pleading shall contain:
1. Short, plain statement of the claim showing entitlement to relief
2. Demand for judgment for relief

### Defenses (CR 8.02)

Defendant shall state defenses to each claim and admit/deny allegations.

### Affirmative Defenses (CR 8.03)

Must be specifically pled:
- Accord and satisfaction
- Arbitration and award
- Assumption of risk
- Comparative fault
- Contributory negligence
- Discharge in bankruptcy
- Estoppel
- Failure of consideration
- Fraud
- Illegality
- Laches
- License
- Payment
- Release
- Res judicata
- Statute of frauds
- Statute of limitations
- Waiver

---

## Civil Rule 10 - Form of Pleadings

### Caption (CR 10.01)

Every pleading shall contain:
- Name of court
- Title of action
- File number (if assigned)
- Designation (Complaint, Answer, etc.)

### Paragraphs (CR 10.02)

- Numbered consecutively
- Each claim/defense in separate count
- Separate statement of circumstances/transactions

### Exhibits (CR 10.03)

- Written instruments may be attached as exhibits
- Become part of the pleading

---

## Civil Rule 11 - Signing of Pleadings

### Signature Required (CR 11)

- Attorney or party must sign
- Signature certifies:
  - Well grounded in fact
  - Warranted by existing law
  - Not for improper purpose

### Sanctions

- Court may impose sanctions for violations

## Venue Rules

### Proper Venue (KRS 452.400 et seq.)

Personal injury actions may be brought in:
- County where defendant resides
- County where cause of action arose
- County where plaintiff resides (if defendant non-resident)

## Filing Requirements

### E-Filing (eFlex)

- All civil cases must be e-filed
- Filing fee required at time of filing
- System assigns case number automatically

### Filing Fees (as of 2024)

| Document | Fee |
|----------|-----|
| Civil Complaint | $150-200 |
| Summons | Included |
| Motions | Varies |
