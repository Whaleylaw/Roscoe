# Cause of Action Templates

## Count I: Negligence

```
COUNT I
NEGLIGENCE

[Paragraph number]. Plaintiff incorporates by reference all preceding
paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Next number]. At all times relevant hereto, Defendant owed a duty
to Plaintiff to [describe duty - e.g., "operate their motor vehicle
in a safe and prudent manner"].

[Next number]. Defendant breached this duty by:
    a. [Specific act or omission];
    b. [Specific act or omission];
    c. [Additional acts as applicable].

[Next number]. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's
negligence, Plaintiff suffered the following injuries and damages:
    a. Physical injuries including [describe];
    b. Pain and suffering, past and future;
    c. Medical expenses, past and future;
    d. Lost wages and earning capacity;
    e. [Additional damages as applicable].

[Next number]. Defendant's conduct was negligent under Kentucky law.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendant for
compensatory damages, costs of this action, and all other relief
to which Plaintiff may be entitled.
```

## Count II: Gross Negligence / Punitive Damages

```
COUNT II
GROSS NEGLIGENCE

[Paragraph number]. Plaintiff incorporates by reference all preceding
paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Next number]. Defendant's conduct as alleged herein was grossly
negligent in that Defendant acted with wanton and reckless disregard
for the safety of others, including Plaintiff.

[Next number]. Specifically, Defendant's grossly negligent conduct
included:
    a. [Describe egregious conduct];
    b. [Additional egregious conduct].

[Next number]. Defendant's grossly negligent conduct demonstrates
a conscious and reckless disregard for the consequences of such
conduct, thereby entitling Plaintiff to punitive damages under
Kentucky law.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendant for
compensatory damages, punitive damages, costs of this action,
and all other relief to which Plaintiff may be entitled.
```

---

## Count III: Negligent Entrustment

```
COUNT III
NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT

[Paragraph number]. Plaintiff incorporates by reference all preceding
paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Next number]. At all times relevant hereto, Defendant [Owner] was
the owner of [describe vehicle/equipment].

[Next number]. Defendant [Owner] entrusted said [vehicle/equipment]
to [Operator] knowing or having reason to know that [Operator] was
incompetent, inexperienced, or reckless in operation thereof.

[Next number]. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant [Owner]'s
negligent entrustment, Plaintiff suffered the injuries and damages
previously alleged.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendant for
compensatory damages, costs of this action, and all other relief
to which Plaintiff may be entitled.
```

---

## Count IV: Vicarious Liability / Respondeat Superior

```
COUNT IV
VICARIOUS LIABILITY

[Paragraph number]. Plaintiff incorporates by reference all preceding
paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Next number]. At all times relevant hereto, [Employee] was acting
within the course and scope of employment with Defendant [Employer].

[Next number]. Defendant [Employer] is vicariously liable for the
negligent acts of [Employee] under the doctrine of respondeat superior.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendant for
compensatory damages, costs of this action, and all other relief
to which Plaintiff may be entitled.
```
