# Affirmative Defenses Reference

## Overview

Affirmative defenses must be specifically pled in the answer (CR 8.03). The defendant bears the burden of proof on affirmative defenses.

---

## Common Personal Injury Defenses

### Comparative Fault (KRS 411.182)

**Definition:** Plaintiff's own negligence contributed to injuries.

**Kentucky Rule:** Pure comparative fault - plaintiff's recovery reduced by their percentage of fault, but not barred unless 100% at fault.

**Discovery Focus:**
- Plaintiff's actions at time of accident
- Any traffic violations by plaintiff
- Distraction, impairment issues

**Response Strategy:**
- Highlight defendant's clear fault
- Minimize plaintiff's contributing actions
- Argue plaintiff's conduct didn't cause injuries

---

### Assumption of Risk

**Definition:** Plaintiff voluntarily encountered known danger.

**Elements:**
1. Knowledge of the risk
2. Appreciation of the risk
3. Voluntary exposure to the risk

**Discovery Focus:**
- What plaintiff knew
- Warnings provided
- Plaintiff's experience/training

**Response Strategy:**
- Plaintiff didn't know of specific risk
- Risk wasn't obvious
- No meaningful choice but to encounter risk

---

### Statute of Limitations

**Definition:** Claim filed too late.

**Kentucky PI SOL:**
- Motor vehicle: 2 years (KRS 304.39-230) - BUT see PIP notice requirements
- General negligence: 1 year (KRS 413.140)
- Discovery rule may toll

**Response Strategy:**
- Verify filing date
- Assert discovery rule if applicable
- Check for tolling (minority, incapacity)

---

### Failure to Mitigate Damages

**Definition:** Plaintiff failed to minimize their damages.

**Common Claims:**
- Didn't follow medical advice
- Delayed treatment
- Refused recommended surgery

**Discovery Focus:**
- Medical records showing non-compliance
- Gaps in treatment

**Response Strategy:**
- Medical reasons for decisions
- Financial barriers to treatment
- Second opinions supported choices

---

### Pre-Existing Condition

**Definition:** Injuries existed before accident.

**Kentucky Rule:** "Eggshell plaintiff" - take plaintiff as you find them. But defendant only liable for aggravation.

**Discovery Focus:**
- Prior medical records
- Prior claims
- Prior accidents

**Response Strategy:**
- New injuries from this accident
- Aggravation of condition
- Causation expert testimony

---

### Intervening/Superseding Cause

**Definition:** Subsequent event broke chain of causation.

**Elements:**
1. Intervening act occurred after defendant's negligence
2. Act was unforeseeable
3. Act was the proximate cause of injury

**Response Strategy:**
- Original negligence set chain in motion
- Intervening act was foreseeable
- Both causes contributed

---

### Sudden Emergency

**Definition:** Defendant faced sudden emergency not of their making.

**Elements:**
1. Emergency arose suddenly
2. Not caused by defendant
3. Defendant acted reasonably under circumstances

**Response Strategy:**
- Emergency was foreseeable
- Defendant created the emergency
- Response was unreasonable

---

## Less Common Defenses

### Release/Prior Settlement

**Action:** Obtain and review any release documents.

### Workers' Compensation Exclusivity

**Action:** Verify employment relationship, determine if exclusive remedy applies.

### Governmental Immunity

**Action:** Research specific immunity provisions for governmental defendants.

### Charitable Immunity

**Action:** Largely abrogated in Kentucky - research current status.
