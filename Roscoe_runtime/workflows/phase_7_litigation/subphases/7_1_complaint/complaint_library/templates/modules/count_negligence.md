# Count Module: Negligence

## Usage

This is the foundational count for most personal injury claims. Include in every complaint unless strict liability or intentional tort is the primary theory.

## Template

```markdown
### COUNT [N]: NEGLIGENCE

[Paragraph Number]. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates all preceding paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.name], owed a duty of care to Plaintiff [describe duty - e.g., "to operate the motor vehicle in a safe and prudent manner" OR "to maintain the premises in a reasonably safe condition" OR "to exercise reasonable care in the performance of services"].

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.name], breached that duty by one or more of the following negligent acts or omissions:

    a. [Specific negligent act 1];
    
    b. [Specific negligent act 2];
    
    c. [Specific negligent act 3];
    
    d. [Additional acts as applicable].

[Paragraph Number]. The negligent acts and omissions of Defendant, [defendant.name], were a substantial factor in causing [describe the incident] and Plaintiff's resulting injuries.

[Paragraph Number]. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff, [plaintiff.name], has suffered one or more of the following damages:

    a. Temporary and permanent bodily injuries;
    
    b. Physical pain and mental suffering, past and future;
    
    c. Past medical expenses and future medical expenses;
    
    d. Lost wages and impairment of ability to labor and earn money;
    
    e. Loss of enjoyment of life;
    
    f. Increased risk of future harm.
```

## Common Negligent Acts by Case Type

### Motor Vehicle Accident
- Failing to maintain a proper lookout
- Failing to keep the vehicle under proper control
- Operating the vehicle at an excessive rate of speed
- Following too closely
- Failing to yield the right of way
- Failing to obey traffic control devices
- Driving while distracted
- Driving while impaired
- Making an improper lane change
- Failing to signal

### Premises Liability
- Failing to maintain the premises in a reasonably safe condition
- Failing to inspect the premises for dangerous conditions
- Failing to remedy the dangerous condition within a reasonable time
- Failing to warn of the dangerous condition
- Allowing the dangerous condition to exist when Defendant knew or should have known
- Failing to have adequate policies and procedures for maintenance

### Professional Negligence
- Failing to exercise reasonable care in the performance of services
- Failing to meet the applicable standard of care
- Failing to properly supervise
- Failing to follow established protocols

## Kentucky Legal Standards

**Elements of Negligence:**
1. Duty - Defendant owed a duty to Plaintiff
2. Breach - Defendant breached that duty
3. Causation - Breach was substantial factor in causing harm
4. Damages - Plaintiff suffered actual damages

**Standard:** Negligence is the failure to exercise the degree of care that an ordinarily prudent person would exercise under similar circumstances.

**Kentucky Pattern Instruction:** See KPI 12.02 (Definition of Negligence)
