# Count Module: Vicarious Liability (Respondeat Superior)

## Usage

Add this count when:
- Negligent actor was an employee
- Employee was acting within scope of employment at time of incident
- Employer should be liable for employee's negligence

**Note:** Often combined with Count for Negligent Hiring/Retention/Supervision.

## Prerequisites

- Must include negligence count against employee
- Must establish employment relationship
- Must establish employee was acting within scope of employment

## Template

```markdown
### COUNT [N]: VICARIOUS LIABILITY - [defendant.employer.name]

[Paragraph Number]. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates all preceding paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Paragraph Number]. At all relevant times, Defendant, [defendant.employee.name], was an employee of Defendant, [defendant.employer.name].

[Paragraph Number]. At the time of the [collision/incident], Defendant, [defendant.employee.name], was acting in the course of and within the scope of employment for Defendant, [defendant.employer.name].

[Paragraph Number]. [AGENT: Include facts showing scope of employment - e.g., "was making deliveries for employer," "was driving employer's vehicle for work purposes," "was traveling between job sites during work hours," "was performing job duties assigned by employer"]

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.employer.name], is vicariously liable for the negligence of its employee, [defendant.employee.name], under the doctrine of *respondeat superior*.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.employer.name], is believed to be the owner of the vehicle driven by [defendant.employee.name] at the time of the negligent acts.

[Paragraph Number]. As a result of the negligence of [defendant.employee.name], for which [defendant.employer.name] is vicariously liable, Plaintiff has suffered the damages set forth above.
```

---

## Scope of Employment Factors

Employment scope is determined by whether employee was:
- Performing duties assigned by employer
- Acting to further employer's business
- Using employer's equipment/vehicle
- During work hours
- At work location or traveling for work

**Outside Scope:**
- Personal errands
- Frolic and detour
- Intentional acts outside job duties
- Commuting to/from work (generally)

## Additional Count: Negligent Hiring/Retention/Supervision

Often added alongside vicarious liability:

```markdown
### COUNT [N]: NEGLIGENT HIRING, RETENTION, AND SUPERVISION

[Paragraph Number]. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates all preceding paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.employer.name], owed a duty to the public, including Plaintiff, to exercise reasonable care in hiring, retaining, and supervising its employees.

[Paragraph Number]. [defendant.employee.name]'s [driving record / background / characteristics] were such that a reasonably prudent employer would have, or should have, known that allowing [defendant.employee.name] to [operate vehicles / perform job duties] would place third persons in danger.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.employer.name], hired and/or retained [defendant.employee.name] without properly:

    a. Conducting a background check;
    
    b. Verifying driving record;
    
    c. Checking references;
    
    d. Assessing qualifications for the position.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.employer.name], failed to properly supervise [defendant.employee.name], including but not limited to:

    a. Failing to monitor employee performance;
    
    b. Failing to enforce safety policies;
    
    c. Ignoring evidence of dangerous behavior;
    
    d. Failing to provide adequate training.

[Paragraph Number]. [defendant.employer.name]'s negligent hiring, retention, and supervision was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff's injuries.
```

## Prayer for Relief Addition

Add to Wherefore clause:

```markdown
[N]. Judgment against Defendants, [defendant.employee.name] and [defendant.employer.name], jointly and severally, for compensatory damages in an amount in excess of the jurisdictional limits of this Court;

[N]. Punitive damages against [defendant.employer.name] for negligent hiring, retention, and supervision, as authorized by law;
```

## Evidence to Gather

- Employment records
- Job description
- Training records
- Driving history (if applicable)
- Previous complaints/incidents
- Company policies and procedures
- GPS/dispatch records
- Time cards/work schedules
