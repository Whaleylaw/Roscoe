# Count Module: Insurance Bad Faith

## Usage

Add this count when an insurance carrier has:
- Denied a valid claim without reasonable basis
- Unreasonably delayed payment or settlement
- Failed to properly investigate
- Failed to make good faith settlement offer
- Misrepresented coverage or policy terms

## Prerequisites

- Must have insurance carrier named as defendant
- Must be able to articulate specific UCSPA violations
- Carrier must be subject to Kentucky insurance regulations

## Template

```markdown
### COUNT [N]: BAD FAITH - [insurance.company]

[Paragraph Number]. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates all preceding paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [insurance.company], is an insurance company licensed to write insurance policies in the Commonwealth of Kentucky and is subject to KRS Chapter 304.

[Paragraph Number]. At all relevant times, [insurance.company] was subject to and required to comply with the Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act, KRS 304.12-230.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [insurance.company], had a duty to deal with [Plaintiff / its insured] in good faith and to comply with the Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [insurance.company], violated KRS 304.12-230 by one or more of the following acts or omissions:

    a. Misrepresenting pertinent facts or insurance policy provisions relating to coverage at issue;
    
    b. Failing to acknowledge and act reasonably and promptly upon communications with respect to claims arising under insurance policies;
    
    c. Failing to adopt and implement reasonable standards for the prompt investigation of claims arising under insurance policies;
    
    d. Refusing to pay claims without conducting a reasonable investigation based upon all available information;
    
    e. Failing to affirm or deny coverage of claims within a reasonable time after proof of loss statements have been completed;
    
    f. Not attempting in good faith to effectuate prompt, fair, and equitable settlements of claims in which liability has become reasonably clear;
    
    g. Compelling insureds to institute litigation to recover amounts due under an insurance policy by offering substantially less than the amounts ultimately recovered;
    
    h. Attempting to settle a claim for less than the amount to which a reasonable person would have believed he was entitled by reference to written or printed advertising material accompanying or made part of an application;
    
    i. Failing to promptly provide a reasonable explanation of the basis in the insurance policy in relation to the facts or applicable law for denial of a claim or for the offer of a compromise settlement;
    
    j. [Add specific violation based on facts].

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [insurance.company]'s conduct was in reckless disregard of the rights of [Plaintiff / its insured].

[Paragraph Number]. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant, [insurance.company]'s bad faith, Plaintiff has suffered consequent and proximate injury, including:

    a. Delay in receiving compensation for injuries;
    
    b. Additional costs in pursuing the claim;
    
    c. Emotional distress and mental anguish;
    
    d. Attorney's fees and litigation costs.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [insurance.company]'s conduct was willful, wanton, and malicious, entitling Plaintiff to punitive damages.
```

## UCSPA Violations Reference

**KRS 304.12-230** prohibits these practices with such frequency as to indicate a general business practice:

1. **Misrepresentation** - Misrepresenting facts or policy provisions
2. **Failure to Acknowledge** - Not responding promptly to communications
3. **No Investigation Standards** - Failing to adopt reasonable investigation procedures
4. **Refusing Without Investigation** - Denying claims without reasonable investigation
5. **Unreasonable Delay** - Failing to affirm/deny within reasonable time
6. **Bad Faith Settlement** - Not attempting good faith settlement when liability is clear
7. **Compelling Litigation** - Offering substantially less than ultimately recovered
8. **Lowball Offers** - Settling for less than reasonable person would expect
9. **No Explanation** - Failing to explain denial or settlement basis

## Evidence to Gather

- Claim file (through discovery)
- Timeline of all communications
- Settlement demands and responses
- Investigation reports
- Adjuster notes
- Prior similar claims handling
- Internal guidelines/manuals
