# Settlement Authority Reference

## Pre-Mediation Authority Discussion

### Topics to Cover with Client

1. **Case Value Range**
   - Best case scenario (high verdict)
   - Realistic scenario (likely verdict)
   - Worst case scenario (defense verdict, costs)

2. **Net to Client Analysis**
   - Gross settlement
   - Attorney fees (percentage)
   - Costs advanced
   - Lien amounts
   - Net to client

3. **Authority Levels**
   - Opening demand
   - Target settlement
   - Bottom line (walk-away number)

## At Mediation: Getting Additional Authority

### When Needed

- Defense offers within range but above authority
- New information changes calculation
- Client reconsidering after seeing defense's position

### How to Ask

```
"The defense is at $X. We discussed $Y as our bottom line. 
Here's why I think we should consider accepting/countering:
[Reasons]. What do you think?"
```

### Factors to Discuss

- Certainty vs. risk
- Time value of money
- Emotional cost of trial
- Case weaknesses revealed
- Defense posture

---

## Documentation

### If Settlement Reached

Document immediately:
- Total settlement amount
- Payment terms
- Release scope
- Confidentiality terms
- Dismissal terms
- Any special provisions

### If Impasse

Document:
- Final positions
- Reason for impasse
- Any continued negotiation terms
- Client's understanding of next steps

---

## Ethical Considerations

### Must Have Authority
- Don't attend without client authority
- Don't agree without client approval
- Keep client informed

### Confidentiality
- Mediation communications confidential
- Can't be used at trial
- Client privilege still applies
