# Mediation Brief Template

## Format

```
                    MEDIATION BRIEF
                    
                [PLAINTIFF NAME] v. [DEFENDANT NAME]
                    Civil Action No. [NUMBER]
                    
                    Mediation Date: [DATE]
                    Mediator: [NAME]

                    SUBMITTED BY PLAINTIFF

I. INTRODUCTION

    This personal injury action arises from a motor vehicle 
accident that occurred on [DATE] in [LOCATION]. [Brief 1-2 
sentence case summary].

II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

    A. The Accident
    
    [Describe how accident occurred, defendant's fault, 
    plaintiff's lack of fault]
    
    B. Plaintiff's Injuries
    
    [Summarize injuries sustained]
    
    C. Treatment Summary
    
    [Brief treatment history]

III. LIABILITY ANALYSIS

    A. Defendant's Negligence
    
    [Set forth liability facts favoring plaintiff]
    
    B. Comparative Fault (if any)
    
    [Address any comparative fault issues]
    
    C. Verdict Potential
    
    [Discuss liability strength]

IV. DAMAGES

    A. Medical Expenses
    
    | Provider | Treatment | Amount |
    |----------|-----------|--------|
    | [Name] | [Treatment] | $[Amount] |
    | [Name] | [Treatment] | $[Amount] |
    | **TOTAL** | | **$[Total]** |
    
    B. Future Medical Expenses
    
    [Estimate if applicable]
    
    C. Lost Wages
    
    [Past and future if applicable]
    
    D. Pain and Suffering
    
    [Describe nature and extent]
    
    E. Total Damages Summary
    
    | Category | Amount |
    |----------|--------|
    | Past Medical | $[Amount] |
    | Future Medical | $[Amount] |
    | Past Lost Wages | $[Amount] |
    | Future Lost Wages | $[Amount] |
    | Pain and Suffering | $[Amount] |
    | **TOTAL** | **$[Amount]** |

V. SETTLEMENT HISTORY

    [Summarize prior negotiations]

VI. SETTLEMENT DEMAND

    Plaintiff demands $[AMOUNT] to settle this matter.
    
    [Brief justification for demand]

VII. CONCLUSION

    Plaintiff is prepared to negotiate in good faith and 
looks forward to a productive mediation session.

                    Respectfully submitted,
                    
                    [ATTORNEY NAME]
                    Counsel for Plaintiff
```

---

## Section-by-Section Guidance

### I. Introduction
- Keep very brief
- Set the stage without advocacy overload
- Mediator wants facts, not argument

### II. Factual Background
- Clear, chronological narrative
- Emphasize defendant's fault
- Don't overstate or exaggerate

### III. Liability Analysis
- Be honest about weaknesses
- Mediator will see through puffery
- Acknowledge comparative fault if present

### IV. Damages
- Be specific with numbers
- Include supporting evidence references
- Distinguish past from future

### V. Settlement History
- Prior demands and offers
- Why not settled previously
- What has changed

### VI. Settlement Demand
- Realistic starting point
- Brief justification
- Room to negotiate

---

## What NOT to Include

- Confidential settlement communications
- Insults to opposing party
- Unrealistic damage claims
- Anything you don't want seen if case doesn't settle
