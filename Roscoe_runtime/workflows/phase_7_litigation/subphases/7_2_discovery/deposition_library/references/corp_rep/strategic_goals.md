# Strategic Goal Identification for CR 30.02(6) Depositions

## Overview

Before drafting a corporate representative deposition notice, identify the primary strategic objectives. Different goals require different topic formulations.

---

## Strategic Goal Matrix

| Goal | When to Use | Topic Focus |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Discovery Mapping** | Early in litigation; need to find documents and witnesses | Document locations, custodians, organizational structure |
| **Compliance Verification** | Discovery responses seem incomplete or evasive | Verification of interrogatory answers, RFP responses |
| **Document Explanation** | Complex documents/databases produced without context | Meaning, creation, usage of specific documents |
| **Liability Pin-Down** | Need to bind corporation to facts before trial | Specific factual admissions, corporate knowledge |
| **ESI Mapping** | Electronic data may be relevant; spoliation concerns | Email systems, retention policies, litigation holds |
| **Foundational Rules** | Want to establish standards corporation must follow | Policies, procedures, training, safety standards |

---

## Goal 1: Discovery Mapping

**Purpose:** Identify where information is located and who has knowledge.

**When to Use:**
- Early in discovery
- Large corporate defendant with complex structure
- Uncertain where relevant documents are maintained
- Need to identify key witnesses for later depositions

**Topic Examples:**
- "The identity and location of all documents relating to [subject]"
- "The persons at [Corporation] with knowledge of [subject]"
- "The organizational structure of [department] responsible for [function]"
- "The systems used to maintain records relating to [subject]"

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- [ ] Where are the documents?
- [ ] Who are the custodians?
- [ ] What systems contain the information?
- [ ] Who has personal knowledge of relevant facts?

---

## Goal 2: Discovery Compliance Verification

**Purpose:** Test whether discovery responses were complete and accurate.

**When to Use:**
- Responses to interrogatories seem incomplete
- RFP responses are vague or potentially incomplete
- Suspicion of missing documents
- Need to verify accuracy of representations

**Topic Examples:**
- "The process by which [Corporation] responded to Plaintiff's First Set of Interrogatories"
- "The search conducted for documents responsive to Plaintiff's First Request for Production"
- "The accuracy and completeness of [Corporation's] response to Interrogatory No. [X]"
- "Any documents responsive to Plaintiff's discovery requests that were withheld"

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- [ ] Were responses complete?
- [ ] What search was conducted?
- [ ] Were any documents withheld?
- [ ] Who prepared the responses?

---

## Goal 3: Document Explanation

**Purpose:** Get official corporate explanation for complex documents or data.

**When to Use:**
- Documents produced are technical or complex
- Database extracts need explanation
- Internal codes or abbreviations need clarification
- Document authenticity or meaning in dispute

**Topic Examples:**
- "The creation, purpose, and meaning of the document marked as Exhibit [X]"
- "The data fields and their meanings in the database extract produced as [document]"
- "The abbreviations and codes used in [document type]"
- "[Corporation's] procedures for creating and maintaining [document type]"

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- [ ] What does this document mean?
- [ ] Who created it and why?
- [ ] What do the codes/abbreviations mean?
- [ ] Is this document accurate?

---

## Goal 4: Liability Pin-Down

**Purpose:** Bind the corporation to specific facts that cannot be changed at trial.

**When to Use:**
- Key facts need to be established
- Want to prevent defendant from changing story at trial
- Need corporate admission on specific points
- Preparing for summary judgment

**Topic Examples:**
- "All facts known to [Corporation] concerning the incident on [date]"
- "[Corporation's] contention regarding [specific fact or allegation]"
- "Whether [Corporation] admits or denies [specific allegation in Complaint]"
- "[Corporation's] knowledge of [hazard/condition] prior to [date]"

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- [ ] What is the corporation's position?
- [ ] What facts does the corporation admit?
- [ ] What facts does the corporation deny?
- [ ] What was known and when?

---

## Goal 5: ESI Mapping

**Purpose:** Understand electronic systems, retention, and preservation.

**When to Use:**
- Electronic communications may be relevant
- Spoliation concerns exist
- Need to understand data systems for targeted requests
- Federal litigation with ESI discovery obligations

**Topic Examples:**
- "All email systems used by [Corporation] during [time period]"
- "All electronic communication systems besides email used by [Corporation]"
- "Backup and archive systems for electronic communications"
- "Retention and destruction policies for electronic data"
- "The litigation hold implemented in connection with this lawsuit"
- "The method of search for ESI responsive to discovery requests"

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- [ ] What systems exist?
- [ ] What are the retention policies?
- [ ] When was litigation hold implemented?
- [ ] How was ESI searched?

---

## Goal 6: Foundational Rules Establishment

**Purpose:** Get corporate agreement to safety standards and rules.

**When to Use:**
- Preparing to establish "rules" before applying to facts
- Want binding corporate admission on standards
- Building foundation for closing argument
- Setting up "any mistakes made?" closing gambit

**Topic Examples:**
- "[Corporation's] policies and procedures regarding [safety topic]"
- "The training provided to [employees] regarding [subject]"
- "[Corporation's] standards for [function/activity]"
- "The safety rules applicable to [activity/operation]"

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- [ ] What rules/standards apply?
- [ ] What training is provided?
- [ ] What policies govern conduct?
- [ ] What does corporation expect of employees?

---

## Multi-Goal Depositions

Often, a single CR 30.02(6) deposition will serve multiple strategic goals. Structure the notice with topics organized by goal:

**Example Structure:**
1. Topics 1-3: Discovery Mapping
2. Topics 4-6: ESI Mapping
3. Topics 7-10: Liability Pin-Down
4. Topics 11-14: Foundational Rules

---

## Goal Selection Checklist

Before drafting notice:

- [ ] What is the primary strategic goal?
- [ ] What secondary goals can be achieved?
- [ ] What specific information is needed?
- [ ] What topics will achieve these goals?
- [ ] Are topics specific enough for "reasonable particularity"?
- [ ] Are topics broad enough to get needed information?
