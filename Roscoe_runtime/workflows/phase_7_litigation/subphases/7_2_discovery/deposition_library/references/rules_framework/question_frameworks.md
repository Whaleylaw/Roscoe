# Question Frameworks by Case Type

## Overview

Use these frameworks as starting points for rules-based questioning. Adapt with case-specific details.

---

## General Framework

### Structure

For each rule:
```
Q. [General principle statement]. Do you agree with that?
Q. [More specific application]. Do you agree?
Q. [Connect to defendant's duty]. Do you agree?
Q. Would violation of this rule be [careless/reckless/dangerous]?
```

### Escalation Words

Progress from less severe to more severe:
1. Careless
2. Negligent
3. Reckless
4. Dangerous

## Motor Vehicle Collision

### Basic Safety Rules

```
Q. All drivers must be attentive and watch where they are going. Do you agree?
Q. All drivers must obey the traffic laws. Do you agree?
Q. These are safety rules. Do you agree?
Q. It would be dangerous if a driver doesn't follow these safety rules. Do you agree?
Q. It would be reckless if a driver doesn't follow these safety rules. Do you agree?
```

### Stop Signs

```
Q. A driver must come to a complete stop at a stop sign. Do you agree?
Q. Especially [a truck driver / a commercial driver]. Do you agree?
Q. Does [Company] expect its drivers to follow this rule?
Q. If a [truck] driver did not follow this rule and ran a stop sign, would you consider that driver to be careless?
Q. Would you consider that driver to be reckless?
```

### Intersection Rules

```
Q. Before pulling into oncoming traffic, a driver should look left, right, and left again. Do you agree?
Q. A driver must yield to vehicles already in the intersection. Do you agree?
Q. A driver who fails to yield creates a dangerous situation. Do you agree?
```

### Lookout Duty

```
Q. A driver must maintain a proper lookout at all times. Do you agree?
Q. That means watching where you're going. Do you agree?
Q. And watching for other vehicles. Do you agree?
Q. A driver who doesn't maintain a proper lookout is being careless. Do you agree?
```

### Distracted Driving

```
Q. A driver must not operate a vehicle while distracted. Do you agree?
Q. Using a cell phone while driving is a distraction. Do you agree?
Q. Texting while driving is dangerous. Do you agree?
Q. A driver who is texting while driving is being reckless. Do you agree?
```

## Trucking / Commercial Vehicle

### Higher Duty

```
Q. A commercial truck driver has a higher duty of care than a regular driver. Do you agree?
Q. That's because a tractor-trailer can cause more damage. Do you agree?
Q. The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations exist to protect the public. Do you agree?
```

### Hours of Service

```
Q. A trucking company must ensure its drivers comply with hours-of-service regulations. Do you agree?
Q. These regulations exist to prevent fatigued driving. Do you agree?
Q. A fatigued driver is a dangerous driver. Do you agree?
Q. A driver who exceeds hours-of-service limits is creating a safety risk. Do you agree?
```

### Qualifications

```
Q. A trucking company must ensure its drivers are properly qualified. Do you agree?
Q. That includes having a valid CDL. Do you agree?
Q. And a clean driving record. Do you agree?
Q. A company that puts an unqualified driver on the road is being reckless. Do you agree?
```

### Pre-Trip Inspections

```
Q. Pre-trip inspections are important for safety. Do you agree?
Q. A driver should check brakes, lights, tires before driving. Do you agree?
Q. A driver who skips pre-trip inspection is being careless. Do you agree?
```

## Premises Liability

### General Safety

```
Q. A business owner is responsible to make sure the inside of a business is safe. Do you agree?
Q. A business owner must not needlessly endanger people. Do you agree?
Q. If a company has two options, the company should choose the safer option whenever reasonably possible. Do you agree?
```

### Inspection

```
Q. A business owner should regularly inspect the premises for hazards. Do you agree?
Q. Especially areas where customers walk. Do you agree?
Q. How often should high-traffic areas be inspected?
Q. A business that doesn't inspect for hazards is being negligent. Do you agree?
```

### Floor Safety

```
Q. A business owner must ensure that floors are kept clean and dry. Do you agree?
Q. A wet floor is a slip hazard. Do you agree?
Q. If there is a spill, it must be cleaned up promptly. Do you agree?
Q. What does "promptly" mean to you?
Q. A business that leaves spills on the floor is creating a dangerous condition. Do you agree?
```

### Warnings

```
Q. If a hazard exists, there must be warning signs. Do you agree?
Q. Or barriers to keep people away from the hazard. Do you agree?
Q. A business that knows about a hazard and doesn't warn customers is being negligent. Do you agree?
```

### Lighting

```
Q. A business owner must ensure that any area where visitors might go is well-lit. Do you agree?
Q. Stairs must be readily visible to everyone. Do you agree?
Q. A step that is not readily visible could be dangerous. Do you agree?
Q. Poor lighting creates a safety hazard. Do you agree?
```

## Nursing Home / Medical Facility

### Resident Safety

```
Q. The safety of nursing home residents must always come first. Do you agree?
Q. A nursing home cannot needlessly endanger the residents. Do you agree?
Q. Residents depend on the staff for their safety. Do you agree?
```

### Fall Prevention

```
Q. A nursing home must take reasonable steps to prevent a resident from falling. Do you agree?
Q. Especially if the resident is considered a fall risk. Do you agree?
Q. If a resident has a history of falls, the nursing staff must review the notes to see if a pattern can be established. Do you agree?
Q. Any fall intervention must be consistently followed. Do you agree?
Q. An intervention that is not followed is worse than no intervention. Do you agree?
```

### Call Response

```
Q. Staff must respond promptly to call lights. Do you agree?
Q. How long should a resident have to wait for a response?
Q. A delayed response could result in a fall. Do you agree?
Q. A nursing home that ignores call lights is being negligent. Do you agree?
```

### Documentation

```
Q. Accurate documentation is essential to resident care. Do you agree?
Q. Staff must document incidents when they occur. Do you agree?
Q. Inaccurate documentation can lead to patient harm. Do you agree?
```

## Impaired Driving / DUI

```
Q. All drivers must be attentive and watch where they are going. Do you agree?
Q. All drivers must obey the traffic laws. Do you agree?
Q. A driver must not drive a vehicle if they are under the influence of drugs or alcohol. Do you agree?
Q. These are safety rules. Do you agree?
Q. It would be dangerous if a driver doesn't follow these safety rules. Do you agree?
Q. It would be reckless if a driver doesn't follow these safety rules. Do you agree?
Q. Driving under the influence shows disregard for the safety of others. Do you agree?
```

## Closing Gambit

### Use at End of Any Deposition

```
Q. Looking back at this incident, were any mistakes made?

[IF YES:]
Q. What mistakes were made?
Q. Why did those mistakes happen?
Q. What has been done to prevent this from happening again?

[IF NO:]
Q. So your conduct was exactly as expected and trained?
Q. If you had to do it all over again, you would do nothing different?
Q. The company's procedures were followed exactly as designed?
Q. And this [injury/accident] still occurred?
```

## Handling Objections

### "Calls for Legal Opinion"

**Response:**
> "I'm asking about agreed-upon standards, customs, guidelines, or principles in [their industry/profession]. I'm not asking for a legal conclusion about this case."

### "Calls for Speculation"

**Response:**
> "I'm asking about the witness's understanding of [safety standards/company policy], not asking them to speculate about anything unknown."

### "Assumes Facts Not in Evidence"

**Response:**
> "I'm asking about general principles first, not assuming any facts about this case."
