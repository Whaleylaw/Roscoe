## Purpose

This checklist ensures all privileged materials are identified and protected before client deposition. Under KRE 503 (attorney-client privilege) and work product doctrine, certain communications and materials are protected from discovery.

---

## Attorney-Client Privilege Review (KRE 503)

### Communications to Review

Review all communications between:
- [ ] Client and attorney (all lawyers at firm)
- [ ] Client and attorney's staff (paralegals, legal assistants)
- [ ] Client and attorney's agents (investigators working for attorney)

### Privilege Criteria Checklist

For each communication, verify:
- [ ] Made between privileged persons (client, attorney, representatives)
- [ ] Made in confidence
- [ ] For the purpose of seeking/providing legal advice
- [ ] Not shared with third parties

## Preparation for Deposition

### Client Instructions

Prepare client to:
- [ ] Not volunteer information about communications with attorney
- [ ] Not discuss legal strategy or attorney advice
- [ ] Wait for objection before answering privilege-related questions
- [ ] Not disclose documents they reviewed with attorney (unless non-privileged)

### Standard Response Language

If asked about privileged matters, client should respond:
> "I've been instructed by my attorney not to answer that question based on attorney-client privilege."

Then wait for attorney instruction.

## Legal References

### KRE 503 - Lawyer-Client Privilege

> A client has a privilege to refuse to disclose and to prevent any other person from disclosing confidential communications made for the purpose of facilitating the rendition of professional legal services to the client.

### CR 30.03(3) - Objection Conduct

Instruction not to answer limited to:
1. Preserve privilege
2. Enforce court limitation
3. Present motion under CR 30.04

### Work Product Doctrine

Attorney mental impressions, conclusions, opinions, and legal theories are protected. Factual work product may be discoverable upon showing of substantial need.
