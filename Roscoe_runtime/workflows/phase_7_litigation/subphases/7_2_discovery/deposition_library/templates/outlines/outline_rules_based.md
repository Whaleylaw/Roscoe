## Outline Structure

This outline follows the **rules-based examination framework**: establish general safety rules and principles BEFORE discussing case-specific facts. This creates a foundation of agreed-upon standards against which the defendant's conduct can be measured.

## Section 2: Rules Establishment (BEFORE FACTS)

**Purpose:** Get witness agreement to general safety principles before applying them to case facts.

### Instructions for Agent

For each rule:
1. State the rule in plain language
2. Ask for agreement ("Do you agree with that?")
3. If agreed, move to next rule
4. If disputed, note for credibility arguments

### Rule Template Format

```
Q. [General principle statement]. Do you agree with that?
Q. [More specific application]. Do you agree?
Q. [Connect to defendant's duty]. Do you agree?
Q. Would violation of this rule be [careless/reckless/dangerous]?
```

## Section 3: Case-Type Specific Question Frameworks

### For MVA Cases

```
Q. A driver must come to a complete stop at a stop sign. Do you agree?
Q. Especially [a truck driver / a commercial driver]. Do you agree?
Q. Does [Company] expect its drivers to follow this rule?
Q. If a driver did not follow this rule and ran a stop sign, would you consider that driver to be careless?
Q. Would you consider that driver to be reckless?
Q. Before pulling into oncoming traffic, a driver should look left, right, and left again. Do you agree?
Q. A driver must maintain a proper lookout at all times. Do you agree?
Q. A driver must not operate a vehicle while distracted. Do you agree?
```

### For Premises Liability Cases

```
Q. A business owner is responsible to make sure the inside of a business is safe. Do you agree?
Q. A business owner must not needlessly endanger people. Do you agree?
Q. A business owner must ensure that any area where an employee or visitor might go is well-lit. Do you agree?
Q. If a hazard exists, there must be warning signs or barriers. Do you agree?
Q. Floors must be kept clean and dry. Do you agree?
Q. If there is a spill, it must be cleaned up promptly. Do you agree?
Q. If a company has two options, the company should choose the safer option whenever reasonably possible. Do you agree?
```

### For Trucking Cases

```
Q. A commercial truck driver has a higher duty of care than a regular driver. Do you agree?
Q. The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations exist to protect the public. Do you agree?
Q. A trucking company must ensure its drivers are properly qualified. Do you agree?
Q. A trucking company must ensure its drivers comply with hours-of-service regulations. Do you agree?
Q. A fatigued driver is a dangerous driver. Do you agree?
Q. Pre-trip inspections are important for safety. Do you agree?
```

### For Nursing Home Cases

```
Q. The safety of nursing home residents must always come first. Do you agree?
Q. A nursing home cannot needlessly endanger the residents. Do you agree?
Q. A nursing home must take reasonable steps to prevent a resident from falling. Do you agree?
Q. Especially if the resident is considered a fall risk. Do you agree?
Q. If a resident has a history of falls, the nursing staff must review the notes to see if a pattern can be established. Do you agree?
Q. Any fall intervention must be consistently followed. Do you agree?
Q. Staff must respond promptly to call lights. Do you agree?
```

## Section 4: Company Policies and Training

**Purpose:** Establish that defendant had its own rules which were violated.

```
Q. Does [Company] have written policies regarding [safety topic]?
Q. What do those policies require?
Q. Were you trained on these policies?
Q. Are all employees required to follow these policies?
Q. What happens if an employee violates these policies?
Q. [Mark policy document as exhibit] Is this [Company's] policy on [topic]?
Q. This policy was in effect on [date of incident]?
```

## Section 6: Prior Incidents / Notice

**Purpose:** Establish defendant knew or should have known of danger.

```
Q. Before [date of incident], had there been any similar incidents?
Q. Were you aware of any complaints about [hazard/condition]?
Q. What was done in response to those complaints?
Q. Is there an incident reporting system?
Q. Were there any incidents involving [similar hazard] in the past [X] years?
```

---

## Section 7: Closing Gambit - "Any Mistakes?"

**Purpose:** Either get admission of wrongdoing or lock in that conduct was intentional.

```
Q. Looking back at this incident, were any mistakes made?

[IF YES:]
Q. What mistakes were made?
Q. Why did those mistakes happen?
Q. What has been done to prevent this from happening again?

[IF NO:]
Q. So your conduct was exactly as expected and trained?
Q. If you had to do it all over again, you would do nothing different?
Q. The company's procedures were followed exactly as designed?
Q. And this [injury/accident] still occurred?
```

## Objection Response Scripts

### "Calls for Legal Opinion"

> "I'm asking about agreed-upon standards, customs, guidelines, or principles in [their industry/profession]. I'm not asking for a legal conclusion."

### "Calls for Speculation"

> "I'm asking about the witness's understanding of [safety standards/company policy], not asking them to speculate about anything."

### "Assumes Facts Not in Evidence"

> "The premise of my question is based on [document/testimony already in record]."
