# Privilege Log Template and Guide

## Overview

When withholding documents based on privilege (attorney-client, work product, etc.), you must provide a privilege log identifying each withheld document with sufficient detail for the court to evaluate the privilege claim.

---

## Privilege Log Format

### Table Format (Recommended)

| Doc # | Date | Author | Recipient(s) | Document Type | Subject Matter | Privilege Asserted | Basis |
|-------|------|--------|--------------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| 1 | [Date] | [Name] | [Name(s)] | [Type] | [Description] | [Privilege] | [Basis] |

---

## Required Information

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Document Number** | Sequential identifier | 1, 2, 3... |
| **Date** | Date document created | 01/15/2025 |
| **Author** | Person who created document | John Smith, Esq. |
| **Recipient(s)** | Person(s) who received document | Jane Doe (client) |
| **Document Type** | Format of document | Email, Letter, Memo, Notes |
| **Subject Matter** | General description (without revealing privileged content) | Legal advice re: settlement |
| **Privilege Asserted** | Specific privilege claimed | Attorney-Client |
| **Basis for Privilege** | Why privilege applies | Confidential communication seeking legal advice |

## Privilege Descriptions

### Attorney-Client Privilege

**Standard Language:**
> This document constitutes a confidential communication between [attorney/law firm] and [client] made for the purpose of providing or obtaining legal advice. The communication was intended to be and was kept confidential.

### Work Product - Ordinary

**Standard Language:**
> This document was prepared by [attorney/representative] in anticipation of litigation. It reflects factual investigation conducted for litigation purposes.

### Work Product - Opinion

**Standard Language:**
> This document was prepared by [attorney] in anticipation of litigation and reflects the mental impressions, conclusions, opinions, and legal theories of counsel. It is protected as opinion work product.

### Joint Defense Privilege

**Standard Language:**
> This document constitutes a communication among counsel for co-parties made pursuant to a joint defense agreement for the purpose of coordinating a common legal defense. It is protected by the attorney-client privilege and joint defense privilege.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Too vague** | "Legal matters" doesn't allow evaluation | Describe subject without revealing privileged content |
| **Missing dates** | Can't determine if privilege applies | Include date for every document |
| **Missing recipients** | Can't evaluate confidentiality | List all recipients |
| **Blanket descriptions** | All entries identical | Individualize each entry |
| **Over-claiming** | Non-privileged documents on log | Review each document carefully |
| **No log at all** | Privilege may be waived | Always prepare log when asserting privilege |

---

## Supplementing the Privilege Log

If additional privileged documents are discovered:
1. Supplement the privilege log promptly
2. Serve supplemental log on opposing counsel
3. Number new entries sequentially continuing from original log

---

## Responding to Challenges

If opposing party challenges privilege:
1. Be prepared to provide documents for in camera review
2. Have supporting affidavit ready
3. Know the elements of each privilege claimed
4. Be prepared to discuss specific documents
