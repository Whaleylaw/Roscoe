# Verification Page Templates

## Overview

Under Kentucky Civil Rule 33.02, interrogatory answers must be signed by the person making them and verified under oath. This requirement ensures the responding party takes personal responsibility for the accuracy of their answers.

---

## Key Requirements

### Who Must Sign
- **Individual party:** The party themselves
- **Corporation:** Officer or authorized representative
- **Partnership:** Partner
- **Association:** Officer or agent

### Notarization Requirements
- Must be notarized (sworn before notary public)
- Notary must be commissioned in the state where signing occurs
- Commission must be current
- Notary seal/stamp required in Kentucky

### Out-of-State Verification
- Party may sign verification in any state
- Must be notarized in state where signed
- Kentucky courts accept out-of-state notarization

---

## Remote Notarization (Kentucky)

Kentucky permits remote online notarization under KRS 423.445. Requirements:
- Notary registered for remote notarization
- Identity verification procedures followed
- Audio-visual recording of notarization
- Notation that notarization was performed remotely

## Common Issues

| Issue | Problem | Solution |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Missing verification** | Responses not valid | Always include verification |
| **Attorney signs instead of client** | Invalid verification | Client must sign interrogatory answers |
| **Expired notary commission** | Invalid notarization | Verify notary commission current |
| **Wrong party signs** | Invalid verification | Ensure correct party signs |
| **Unsigned verification** | Incomplete response | Get signature before serving |

---

## Practice Tips

1. **Prepare early:** Draft verification and send to client for signature before deadline
2. **Allow time:** Client signature and notarization can take several days
3. **Remote options:** Consider remote notarization for out-of-town clients
4. **Keep copies:** Retain signed original verification
5. **Deadline management:** Track client signature as separate deadline item
