# Deposition Planning Skill

## Overview

Develop comprehensive deposition strategy and outlines for effective testimony gathering.

## When to Use

Use when:
- Preparing to take defendant's deposition
- Planning corporate 30(b)(6) deposition
- Deposing fact or expert witnesses

DO NOT use if:
- Preparing client for their deposition (use deposition-defense)
- Responding to deposition notice

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define Objectives

What do we need from this witness?
- Liability admissions
- Damage evidence
- Witness credibility assessment
- Pin down testimony

### Step 2: Review Available Information

Before drafting outline:
- Discovery responses
- Documents produced
- Pleadings (complaint, answer)
- Prior statements

**See:** `references/depo-outline.md` for outline structure.

### Step 3: Organize by Topic

Standard PI deposition topics:
1. Background
2. Pre-accident events
3. Accident facts
4. Post-accident actions
5. Insurance/investigation
6. Contentions

### Step 4: Prepare Exhibits

- Mark all exhibits in advance
- Organize in order of use
- Have copies for all parties

**See:** `references/exhibit-management.md` for logistics.

## Output Format

```markdown
## Deposition Outline: [Witness Name]

**Date:** [Date]
**Location:** [Location]
**Objectives:** [Key goals]

### Topic 1: [Topic Name]
- [Question area]
- [Follow-up areas]
- **Exhibit:** [Number]

### Topic 2: [Topic Name]
...
```
