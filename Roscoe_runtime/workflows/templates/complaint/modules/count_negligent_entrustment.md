# Count Module: Negligent Entrustment

## Usage

Add this count when:
- Vehicle owner is different from driver
- Owner entrusted vehicle to driver
- Owner knew or should have known driver was unfit
- Driver was inexperienced, careless, or reckless

**Distinguished from Vicarious Liability:**
- Vicarious Liability = employer/employee relationship
- Negligent Entrustment = no employment relationship required, based on entrustment of dangerous instrumentality to unfit person

## Prerequisites

- Must include negligence count against driver
- Must establish owner/entruster as separate defendant
- Must establish owner's knowledge of driver's unfitness

## Template

```markdown
### COUNT [N]: NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT - [defendant.owner.name]

[Paragraph Number]. Plaintiff re-alleges and incorporates all preceding paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

[Paragraph Number]. At all relevant times, Defendant, [defendant.owner.name], was the owner of the vehicle operated by Defendant, [defendant.driver.name], on [accident.date].

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.owner.name], negligently and carelessly entrusted the vehicle to Defendant, [defendant.driver.name].

[Paragraph Number]. At the time of entrustment, Defendant, [defendant.owner.name], knew or should have known that [defendant.driver.name] was inexperienced, careless, or reckless, and that entrusting the vehicle to [defendant.driver.name] would create an unreasonable risk of harm to others.

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.owner.name], knew or should have known of [defendant.driver.name]'s unfitness to operate a motor vehicle based on the following:

    a. [AGENT: Include specific facts showing owner's knowledge - e.g., prior accidents, suspended/revoked license, DUI history, observed reckless behavior, lack of valid license, known substance abuse, prior warnings about driving]

[Paragraph Number]. Under Kentucky law, a vehicle owner who entrusts a vehicle to another whom the owner knows to be inexperienced, careless, or reckless is liable for the natural and probable consequences of the entrustment. *See McGrew v. Stone*, 998 S.W.2d 5 (Ky. 1999); *Brady v. B&B Ice Co.*, 242 Ky. 138 (1931).

[Paragraph Number]. Defendant, [defendant.owner.name]'s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff's injuries.

[Paragraph Number]. As a result of Defendant, [defendant.owner.name]'s negligent entrustment, Plaintiff has suffered the damages set forth herein.
```

## Knowledge Elements

Owner's knowledge can be established by:

**Actual Knowledge:**
- Owner was told of driver's poor driving
- Owner witnessed reckless driving
- Owner knew of prior accidents
- Owner knew of license status

**Constructive Knowledge (Should Have Known):**
- Driver was minor with no experience
- Public record of suspended license
- Family member with known history
- Obvious impairment at time of entrustment

---

## Common Scenarios

| Scenario | Knowledge Element |
|----------|-------------------|
| Parent lends car to teen with accidents | Prior accidents known to parent |
| Friend lends car to person drinking | Visible impairment |
| Owner lends to person with DUI history | Knowledge of DUI conviction |
| Employer provides vehicle to employee | Should have checked driving record |
| Loan to person with suspended license | Should have verified valid license |

## Evidence to Gather

- Vehicle registration/title
- Driver's license status at time of accident
- Driver's driving history (accidents, violations)
- Relationship between owner and driver
- Prior conversations about driver's driving
- Insurance declarations
- Any prior lending of vehicle
