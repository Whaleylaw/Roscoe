## Opening: Establish Record for Trial Use

```
Q. You understand this deposition is being taken for use at trial?
Q. You received our notice indicating this deposition may be used as evidence?
Q. You've been given the opportunity to prepare fully for this examination?
```

## Section 1: Qualifications and Background

**Purpose:** Establish credentials for later "credential framing" - using their expertise against them.

```
Q. Please state your full name and professional address.
Q. What are your educational credentials?
Q. What is your medical specialty / area of expertise?
Q. Are you board certified? In what areas?
Q. How long have you been practicing in this specialty?
Q. Do you have any teaching positions?
Q. Have you published in your field?
```

### Credential Framing Setup

```
Q. So you have [X] years of experience in [specialty]?
Q. You've treated thousands of patients with [condition type]?
Q. You're familiar with the standards of care in [specialty]?
Q. A doctor with your credentials should know [foundational fact about specialty]?
```

---

## Section 2: Compensation and Bias

```
Q. Who retained you in this case?
Q. When were you retained?
Q. What are you being paid for your services?
Q. What is your hourly rate for deposition testimony?
Q. What is your hourly rate for trial testimony?
Q. What percentage of your professional time is devoted to medical-legal work?
Q. Approximately how many times have you been retained by [defense firm/insurance company]?
Q. Do you have a business relationship with [firm/company]?
Q. What percentage of your referrals come from defense work versus plaintiff work?
```

---

## Section 3: Retention and Opinion Formation Timeline

**Purpose:** Expose "snap judgments" - opinions formed before reviewing all materials.

```
Q. When were you first contacted about this case?
Q. When did you first form an opinion?
Q. What materials had you reviewed at that time?
Q. What materials did you receive after forming your initial opinion?
Q. Did any of those materials change your opinion?
Q. [If not] So your opinion was essentially fixed before you saw [late-received materials]?
```

## Section 4: Materials Reviewed

```
Q. What materials did you review in forming your opinions?
Q. Did you review [specific record not listed]?
Q. Were there any materials you requested but did not receive?
Q. Were there any materials you felt were missing?
Q. Did defense counsel provide you with a case summary or analysis?
Q. Did you rely on that summary?
```

## Section 5: The Examination (for DME/IME)

```
Q. How long did you spend with [Plaintiff]?
Q. Describe the examination you performed.
Q. What tests did you perform?
Q. What tests did you NOT perform?
Q. Is [test X] the gold standard for diagnosing [condition]?
Q. Why didn't you perform that test?
Q. Did you take a complete history?
Q. Did you ask about [specific symptom]?
Q. Did you ask about [activity limitation]?
```

---

## Section 6: Opinions - Establishing Rules First

**Purpose:** Get agreement on general principles before challenging specific opinions.

### General Principles

```
Q. Do you agree that [general medical/scientific principle]?
Q. Is it accepted in your field that [standard of care principle]?
Q. Would you agree that [authoritative textbook] is a reliable source in your field?
Q. [Read passage from textbook] Do you agree with that statement?
```

### Then Apply to Case

```
Q. Now let's apply that principle to this case.
Q. Your opinion is that [expert's opinion], correct?
Q. How do you reconcile that with [principle you established]?
Q. How do you reconcile that with [passage from textbook]?
```

---

## Section 7: Squaring Up - Prior Inconsistent Statements

**Purpose:** Confront expert with prior testimony that contradicts current opinions.

### Format

```
Q. You've testified in other cases, correct?
Q. In [Case Name], you gave deposition testimony?
Q. Let me read from page [X], lines [Y-Z]: "[READ EXACT QUOTE]"
Q. Did I read that correctly?
Q. You were under oath when you gave that testimony?
Q. Is that still your opinion today?
Q. [If different] What changed?
Q. [If same] How do you square that with your opinion in this case?
```

## Section 8: Learned Treatise Examination (KRE 803(18))

**Purpose:** Use authoritative texts to contradict expert.

```
Q. Are you familiar with [Publication]?
Q. Is it considered authoritative in your field?
Q. Do you agree it's a reliable source?
Q. Let me read from page [X]: "[READ PASSAGE]"
Q. Do you agree with that statement?
Q. [If disagrees] So your opinion differs from this authoritative text?
Q. [If agrees] How do you reconcile that with your opinion in this case?
```

## Section 9: Conflict Mapping by Juror Archetype

### For Tribal Jurors (anti-establishment)

Focus on:
- "Hired gun" evidence (compensation, frequency of defense work)
- Snap judgments (opinion before seeing all records)
- Common sense violations
- Brief examination time vs. treating physician relationship

### For Moderate Jurors (trust authority)

Focus on:
- Violations of published standards
- Deviation from authoritative treatises
- Contradictions with their own prior testimony
- Failure to follow standard diagnostic protocols

### For Progressive Jurors (pro-science)

Focus on:
- Scientific methodology flaws
- Failure to consider all data
- Cherry-picking favorable findings
- Conflict with peer-reviewed literature
