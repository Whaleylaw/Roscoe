## Opening: Witness Designation Confirmation

**CRITICAL:** Establish on the record that the witness is properly designated and testimony binds the corporation.

```
Q. Please state your name for the record.
Q. You are here today as the corporate representative of [Corporation], correct?
Q. You were designated by [Corporation] to testify on its behalf today?
Q. You understand that your testimony today will bind [Corporation]?
Q. You have been prepared to testify on the topics listed in the deposition notice?
```

### Topic-by-Topic Confirmation

```
Q. Let's go through each topic. Are you prepared to testify on behalf of [Corporation] regarding [Topic 1]?
Q. Are you prepared to testify on behalf of [Corporation] regarding [Topic 2]?
[Continue for each noticed topic]
```

**If witness claims not prepared on a topic:**
```
Q. So [Corporation] is unable to answer questions about [Topic X] today?
Q. Despite receiving notice of this deposition with that topic listed?
Q. And [Corporation] did not designate anyone else to testify on that topic?
```

## Section 2: Topic-by-Topic Examination

### Instructions

For each noticed topic:
1. Reference the specific topic number from the notice
2. Ask foundational questions
3. Exhaust the topic before moving to the next
4. Document any "I don't know" responses

### Topic Template

```
Q. I'm now going to ask you questions about Topic [#] from the deposition notice, which concerns [topic description].

[Specific questions for this topic]

Q. Is there anything else [Corporation] knows about [topic] that you haven't told me?
Q. Are there any other persons at [Corporation] with knowledge of [topic]?
Q. Are there any documents related to [topic] that haven't been produced?
```

## Section 3: Strategic Objective-Specific Questions

### A. Discovery Mapping

**Purpose:** Identify documents, custodians, and information locations.

```
Q. Who at [Corporation] has knowledge of [subject]?
Q. Where are documents relating to [subject] maintained?
Q. What databases or systems contain information about [subject]?
Q. Who is the custodian of records for [document type]?
Q. Has [Corporation] searched for documents responsive to our discovery requests?
Q. Describe the search that was conducted.
Q. Were any documents not produced based on objections?
```

### B. Discovery Compliance Verification

**Purpose:** Test whether discovery responses were complete and accurate.

```
Q. You provided interrogatory answers in this case, correct?
Q. Who participated in preparing those answers?
Q. Let me show you Interrogatory No. [X] and your response.
Q. Is this response complete and accurate?
Q. Is there anything you would add or change?
Q. Your response to RFP No. [X] stated [response]. Is that complete?
```

### C. Liability Pin-Down

**Purpose:** Bind corporation to specific facts for trial.

```
Q. Does [Corporation] agree that [fact statement]?
Q. Is it [Corporation's] position that [defense theory]?
Q. Does [Corporation] contend that [plaintiff allegation] is false?
Q. What is [Corporation's] understanding of what happened on [date]?
Q. Does [Corporation] take any responsibility for [incident]?
```

### D. ESI Mapping

**Purpose:** Understand electronic data systems and retention.

```
Q. What email systems does [Corporation] use?
Q. What versions were in use during [relevant period]?
Q. What is the retention policy for emails?
Q. Are emails backed up? If so, how?
Q. What other electronic communication systems does [Corporation] use?
Q. What is the retention policy for each?
Q. When did [Corporation] implement a litigation hold for this case?
Q. What does the litigation hold cover?
```

## Section 6: Incident Investigation

```

Q. Who conducted the investigation?
Q. When did the investigation begin?
Q. What did the investigation conclude?
Q. Was any report prepared?
Q. [Mark investigation report as exhibit if available]

```

## Section 7: "Know-Nothing" Witness Handling

If witness cannot answer questions on a noticed topic:

```
Q. You were designated by [Corporation] to testify about [topic], correct?
Q. And you cannot answer my questions about [topic]?
Q. Did [Corporation] make any effort to identify someone who could answer?
Q. Are there documents that would have information about [topic]?
Q. Has [Corporation] searched for such documents?
Q. So [Corporation's] position is that it simply doesn't know [topic information]?
```

**Strategic Note:** A "know-nothing" witness on a noticed topic is often more valuable than a prepared witness. Document the failure thoroughly for trial.

## Red Flags

| Red Flag | Response |
|----------|----------|

| Excessive objections | Note pattern; consider motion to compel |
| Instructions not to answer | Challenge unless privilege/court order |
| Witness not actually designated | Stop; demand properly designated witness |
| Document dump at deposition | Object; note inability to review |
